# Cigna Global Health

## 1. Introduction

Cigna is a major international health insurer of American origin, known worldwide for its extensive global network of healthcare providers. This service comes at a price, with Cigna's premiums generally being higher than those of other providers of similar health insurance.

## 2. Coverage, Features, and Important Considerations

### Coverages

*   Medical expenses
*   Dental care

### Features

*   Suitable for all nationalities.
*   Locals cannot be insured on the policy.
*   Maximum 180 days of coverage in the country of origin.
*   Employment is not a prerequisite for applying for this insurance.
*   The policy duration is unlimited (no maximum age applies).
*   There is no maximum age for applying for the insurance.
*   No medical examination is required upon application.
*   Application can be done entirely online.
*   Premium surcharges apply for installment payments (semi-annual, quarterly, or monthly).
*   Cancellable with 14 days' notice. (Interim termination is not possible if a claim has been submitted in the relevant insurance year.)
*   Cigna offers many options for coverage, deductibles, and co-payment, allowing you to customize the insurance to your preferences.
*   Medical costs arising from a war or civil unrest situation are not covered, even if you do not actively participate in the situation.
*   Cigna has a large international network. For planned hospital admissions, Cigna must be informed in advance, and in many cases, Cigna can settle medical costs directly with the hospital, meaning you do not have to pre-finance large amounts.

### Important Notes

The following conditions and rules are also important during the policy term:

*   Interim termination is not possible if a claim has been submitted in the relevant insurance year.
*   Cigna significantly increases premiums annually, with observed increases of over 10%.
*   If an insured person wishes to reduce coverage, health questions will be asked to assess the request. A request to reduce coverage may therefore be rejected.
*   If an insured person wishes to increase coverage, health questions will also be asked to assess the request. The request to increase coverage may be rejected by the insurer.
*   Upon relocation to another country, existing policies may be suddenly canceled due to local regulations.
*   Significant attention is paid to potential pre-existing conditions when claims are submitted. The consequences of suspected concealment of a pre-existing condition can be severe.

### Countries Where Coverage is Not Possible

Cigna may not offer insurance for certain countries. This will be indicated in the online module if applicable to your country of residence.

### Explanation of Free Choice, Private Clinics, and Access to Care

Cigna offers partial free choice of healthcare provider and access to private clinics. However, if you do not use a healthcare provider from the Cigna Network, only 80% of the costs will be reimbursed (not full reimbursement).

Furthermore, according to policy conditions, you must always contact Cigna for "pre-approval" prior to a medical consultation or hospital admission. You can contact Cigna by phone for this. This allows Cigna to assess the necessity of a particular treatment, check if the healthcare provider is in their network (for direct payment reasons, among others), and evaluate if the provider is suitable for the treatment or consultation. This "pre-approval" process is also in your interest but adds an extra step to obtaining care. In comparison, other insurers use a similar "pre-approval" system but apply it exclusively to planned hospital admissions. With Cigna, you must contact the insurer even for a general practitioner consultation.

### Explanation of Pregnancy and Childbirth Coverage

Cigna offers coverage for pregnancy and childbirth in its two highest coverage levels (Gold and Platinum). A waiting period of 10 months always applies from the insurance start date, during which Cigna does not reimburse costs related to pregnancy or childbirth. A C-section is only covered if performed for medical reasons. Reimbursements for both regular and complicated deliveries are capped by Cigna, and the maximum amounts are not high. This low reimbursement means that in countries with relatively high medical costs (USA, Hong Kong, Singapore, Switzerland, etc.), the coverage may not be sufficient, and you may have to pay a (large) portion of the delivery costs yourself.

### Explanation of USA Coverage

The USA has very high medical costs. Healthcare expenses are so high that insurers apply separate conditions and premiums for the USA. With Cigna, if the USA is not included as a covered region on the policy, there is never coverage for (emergency) medical matters in America, not even for a vacation in the USA. If you have included a stay in the USA, it is important to use Cigna's network within the USA. If you do not, you will pay 20% of the medical costs yourself.

### Explanation of Separate 'Medical Evacuation' Module

Cigna offers a separate module called 'medical evacuation' in addition to its health coverage. This can be confusing because the inpatient coverage, which is the mandatory basic coverage of the insurance, already includes a component for ambulance transport, even by plane or helicopter, to a hospital within a 160 km radius. The separate 'medical evacuation' module provides the right to be transported back to your country of origin in case of a medical emergency where you cannot receive adequate local care. It also covers the transport of your remains, should you unfortunately pass away, and transport costs to and from family in case of a medical emergency within the family. The premium for this module is quite expensive.

## 3. Premium

The premium depends on factors such as age, chosen insurance plans, coverage, deductible, and region.

Information on deductibles, co-payment, and out-of-pocket maximum can be found in the Customer Guide.

### Installment Payment (Monthly or Annually)

Cigna applies a surcharge for installment payments. This surcharge is relatively high on a monthly basis (10%). If your financial situation allows, annual payment is therefore more advantageous. Please note that this affects interim termination of the policy. With annual payment, Cigna may decide not to provide a refund upon interim termination if medical costs have been incurred.

## 4. How to Apply for the Insurance?

For a complete understanding of how the insurance works, it is advised to review the Customer Guide and policy conditions.

The Cigna insurance can be applied for online. During the application process, you must answer a number of health questions, based on which the insurer will assess whether they will insure you and, if so, under what conditions. Exclusion of certain conditions may occur, as well as an adjustment of premium and/or deductible.

You can receive a €50 discount if you purchase your Cigna insurance through JoHo Insurances.

## 5. Services After the Insurance Has Started

### Claiming Costs

*   After being accepted by Cigna for the insurance, you will receive login details for your personal online environment where you can find your coverage information.
*   You can choose to pay medical bills yourself and be reimbursed by Cigna afterward, or Cigna also offers the option to email them unpaid bills, after which Cigna arranges payment with the healthcare provider.

More information can be found in the Customer Guide.

### Cancellability of the Insurance

This insurance can be canceled at any time with a 7-day notice period.
