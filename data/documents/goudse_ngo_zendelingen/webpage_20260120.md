# Goudse NGO/Missionary Package

The Goudse NGO Missionary Package is designed for Dutch and Belgians working long-term in less developed and poorer countries. This includes working for an NGO, as a missionary, as an employee of a local organization, as an entrepreneur, or as a volunteer. The insurance covers healthcare, accidents, liability, household contents, and travel, among other things. You are also insured during temporary stays in Belgium or the Netherlands.

## 1. Characteristics of Goudse NGO Missionary Package

The Goudse NGO Missionary Package is a comprehensive insurance package developed for individuals living and working long-term in low-income countries. The package is suitable for situations involving local income or volunteer work, such as:

*   Working for an NGO with a local salary
*   Missionary work
*   Activities like teaching, medical care, or aid work for a local salary
*   Founders or owners of foundations focused on development and sustainability

**Characteristics:**

*   The duration of this package is up to a maximum age of 72.
*   The minimum duration of the package is 1 year.
*   The insurance package is renewed annually on January 1st. New premiums and conditions, if applicable, also take effect on January 1st.
*   The non-life insurances in this package (household contents, liability, etc.) can only be taken out in combination with the health insurance in this package.

**Coverage Overview**

For a detailed overview, please refer to the premium and coverage overview document.

**Explanation of Unrest Areas and Molestation Situations (e.g., war, unrest, riots)**

*   Molestation is covered under healthcare (including medical repatriation) if a molestation situation arises in the country of residence.
*   De Goudse reserves the right to terminate molestation coverage in exceptional situations, with a notice period of 14 days (medical) and 45 days (SOS). For further details, refer to the extensive terms and conditions.
*   There is no coverage if traveling to a country/region for which the Ministry of Foreign Affairs has issued a code red.

**Explanation of Disability Insurance**

Within this package, you can also apply for disability coverage. This component provides you with a replacement income if you are unable to work for an extended period (or permanently) due to illness or accident. There is a standard waiting period of 1 year before any benefit is paid. Application for this component is based on a quote.

To determine the premium, we need the following information:

*   Detailed description of activities;
*   Age;
*   Gross income;

Based on this, we can prepare a quote.

Note: Disability insurance in Africa is limited (upon request).

**Explanation of Pregnancy and Childbirth**

*   In the first year of insurance, a maximum of €2,500 will be reimbursed for pregnancy and childbirth. In the second and subsequent years, pregnancy and childbirth are covered at cost price.
*   The choice of hospital is made in consultation with and subject to approval by De Goudse / Alarm Center.
*   The deductible also applies to these costs.

**Abortion**

*   Contrary to what is stated in the policy conditions regarding abortion, voluntary abortion is also covered.

**Explanation of End Age**

For the healthcare component, limited coverage applies to the age category of 67-72 years. See the terms and conditions for more details.

## 4. How to Apply for the Insurance?

The Goudse NGO/Missionary package can be applied for using the Application Form Goudse NGO/Missionary Package.

The completed forms can be sent by email to:

*   JoHo Insurances - contact@johoinsurances.org
*   Subject: Application Goudse NGO Missionary Package

Important! Do not forget to include a copy of your passport. Instructions on how to safely send your passport copy are available.

If you have problems sending the application by email, you may also send it by post. Processing is then expected to take 2-3 days longer.

**Explanation of Application Form**

On the application form, you fill in your personal details and the desired coverage(s).

**Explanation of Health Declaration and Medical Selection**

For the healthcare component, a health declaration must be completed for medical acceptance. If an Expatriate is seconded by an organization, a 'fit for assignment' declaration may suffice. This means that if the Expatriate (and their family) is found 'fit for assignment' during a medical examination by a doctor, De Goudse will follow this judgment and will not require a health declaration.
