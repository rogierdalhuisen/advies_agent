# Zendelingenpakket Premium and Coverage Overview 2026

## 1. Medical Costs

This chapter outlines the comprehensive health insurance coverage, including benefit limits based on age and geography, as well as specific reimbursements for clinical and outpatient care.

The health insurance provides coverage for medical costs with specific limits based on age and location.

### General coverage limits and conditions

This topic specifies the maximum insured amounts and how these thresholds change significantly for insured persons aged 67 and older or those in the United States.

The stated insured amounts apply per insured person per insurance year, unless stated otherwise. The maximum reimbursement for insured persons aged 0 to 66 inclusive is **€ 1,500,000** per illness. Different limits apply to insured persons aged 67 and older: **€ 100,000** per insured person per illness, **€ 150,000** per insured person in the event of an accident, and **€ 150,000** per insured person per illness in the United States. If an event begins before the 67th birthday and continues thereafter, the lower maximum applies to the costs incurred from the 67th birthday onwards.

### Hospitalization

This topic details the reimbursement policy for staying in a hospital and undergoing medical treatment during admission.

Coverage for stay and treatment in a hospital. These costs are reimbursed at **100%**.

### Medical specialist

This topic explains the coverage provided for consultations and specialized procedures performed by medical specialists.

The costs for treatments by a medical specialist. These costs are reimbursed at **100%**.

### Medicines

This topic outlines the reimbursement rules for pharmaceutical products and prescribed medications.

Reimbursement for prescribed medication. These costs are reimbursed at **100%**.

### Homeopathic medicines

This topic specifies the coverage limits and conditions for alternative homeopathic treatments and medications.

Coverage for homeopathic medication. These costs are reimbursed at **100%** up to a maximum of **€ 200**.

### Patient transport by ambulance

This topic covers the financial aspects of emergency transport services for sick or injured insured persons.

The transport of a sick person by ambulance. These costs are reimbursed at **100%**.

### General practitioner

This topic defines the coverage for primary care services, including consultations and standard treatments at a GP clinic.

The costs for consultations and treatments at the general practitioner (GP). These costs are reimbursed at **100%**.

### Medical aids

This topic refers to the specific regulations governing the reimbursement of medical equipment and supporting aids.

Coverage for medical aids. Reimbursement for this takes place according to the medical aids regulations.

### Kidney dialysis

This topic confirms the full reimbursement status for essential kidney dialysis treatments.

The costs for kidney dialysis treatments. These costs are reimbursed at **100%**.

### Psychiatric care (inpatient)

This topic outlines the maximum duration of stay covered for psychiatric treatment within a clinical institution.

Coverage for psychiatric care with admission to a psychiatric institution. These costs are reimbursed for a maximum of **120 days**.

### Psychological or psychiatric care (outpatient)

This topic specifies the financial limit for mental health care services that do not require clinical admission.

The costs for psychological or psychiatric care without admission to an institution. These costs are reimbursed up to a maximum of **€ 2,000**.

### Psychological support (without indication)

This topic details the coverage for preventative or elective psychological support where a formal medical diagnosis is absent.

Coverage for psychological support for which there is no medical indication. These costs are reimbursed at **100%** up to a maximum of **€ 300**.

### Alternative medicine

This topic sets out the limits on the number of consultations and the maximum price per treatment for alternative therapies.

Reimbursement for alternative treatment methods. Coverage applies to a maximum of **20 consultations** or treatments, with a maximum of **€ 30** per consultation or treatment.

### Midwife/Obstetrician

This topic covers the costs associated with professional assistance during pregnancy and the pre-birth period.

The costs for obstetric assistance. These costs are reimbursed at **100%**.

### Admission to hospital/maternity unit (with medical indication)

This topic explains the coverage for clinical birth settings when deemed medically necessary by a physician.

The costs for admission to a hospital or maternity unit when there is a medical indication. These costs are reimbursed at **100%**.

### Admission to hospital/maternity unit (without medical indication)

This topic outlines the reimbursement restrictions for choosing a hospital birth in the absence of a medical requirement.

The costs for admission to a hospital or maternity unit without a medical indication. The reimbursement is **€ 125** per day for a maximum of **8 days**, or **100%** in countries where a home birth is not possible.

### Childbirth

This topic defines the scope of coverage for costs directly emerging from the act of delivering a child.

The costs directly related to childbirth. These costs are reimbursed at **100%**.

### Childbirth coach

This topic specifies the maximum reimbursement available for the services of a professional childbirth coach.

The costs for a childbirth coach. These are reimbursed at **100%** up to a maximum of **€ 200**.

### Maternity care (Kraamzorg)

This topic explains the calculation for maternity care benefits, accounting for time already spent in the hospital.

The costs for maternity care. There is a reimbursement of **€ 1,500**, less **€ 125** multiplied by the number of days spent in the hospital.

### Travel costs for childbirth in the Netherlands

This topic outlines the percentage of travel expenses covered for women choosing to return to the Netherlands for delivery.

Reimbursement for travel costs if one chooses to give birth in the Netherlands. **75%** of the flight costs for the woman are reimbursed; if there are return flight costs for the baby, these are reimbursed at **100%**.

### Pregnancy and childbirth in the first 9 months

This topic describes the specific financial caps applied to pregnancy-related costs during the initial waiting period of the policy.

Coverage for pregnancy costs in the first nine months after the start date of the insurance. A maximum reimbursement of **€ 2,500** applies; from month 10, coverage applies according to the regular sections.

### Fertility treatments

Coverage for treatments aimed at fertility. These costs are **not covered**.

### Abortion

This topic confirms that abortion procedures are not included in the scope of this insurance coverage.

Coverage for abortion procedures. These costs are **not covered**.

The costs for physiotherapy and exercise therapy. Coverage includes **15 treatments** per indication.

The costs for speech therapy and phoniatrics. These costs are reimbursed at **100%**.

### Phlebologist

### Occupational therapy

### Camouflage therapy

Reimbursement for podiatric insoles. These are reimbursed at **100%**, once every three years.

### Psoriasis day treatment

Coverage for day treatment of psoriasis. **20 treatments** are reimbursed.

### Stuttering therapy

The costs for stuttering therapy. These costs are reimbursed at **100%** up to a maximum of **€ 200**.

### Non-clinical rehabilitation

This topic outlines the coverage for rehabilitation services that do not require an overnight stay in a medical facility.

The costs for rehabilitation without clinical admission. These are reimbursed at **100%** up to a maximum of **€ 2,500**.

### Clinical rehabilitation

### Foreign coverage and interim stay in the Netherlands

The validity of the coverage abroad and during an interim stay in the Netherlands. For both situations, a **100%** reimbursement applies.

### Organ transplants in case of accidents

This topic specifies the coverage for transplant procedures required as a direct result of an accidental injury.

The costs for organ transplants (including the donor) as a result of accidents. These costs are reimbursed at **100%**.

### Second opinion

The costs for consulting a second physician for an assessment. This is reimbursed at **100%**.

### Preventive screening

This topic lists the specific types of cancer and cardiovascular screenings that are fully covered by the plan.

The costs for preventive medical examinations. There is **100%** coverage for preventive screening for cervical, colon, and breast cancer, examination of the heart and blood vessels, and prostate examination.

### Travel vaccinations and Malaria prophylaxis

This topic defines the maximum reimbursements for preventative travel medicine and required vaccinations.

Reimbursement for recommended vaccinations and prevention. Recommended travel vaccinations are reimbursed at **100%** up to a maximum of **€ 100**, and Malaria prophylaxes are reimbursed at **100%** up to a maximum of **€ 150**.

### Sterilization

This topic confirms the full coverage for elective sterilization procedures.

The costs for sterilization. This procedure is reimbursed at **100%**.

### Accommodation costs (in case of illness)

This topic outlines the daily allowance and maximum limits for lodging required due to a medical event.

Reimbursement for accommodation costs in connection with illness. Coverage is **€ 100** per day for a maximum of **2 persons**, up to a maximum of **€ 2,000** per event.

### Home nursing

This topic details the daily hour limits and maximum number of days allowed for professional nursing care at home.

The costs for home nursing by a qualified nurse. The reimbursement applies to a maximum of **8 hours per day** up to a maximum of **100 days**.

### Repatriation (Air travel costs)

This topic covers the costs for emergency medical transport back to the home country via air.

Costs for repatriation by scheduled or ambulance aircraft. These costs are reimbursed at **100%**.

### Repatriation for pregnancy complications

This topic specifies the coverage for air transport required due to emergency issues during a pregnancy.

Repatriation in connection with emergency complications during pregnancy. These costs are reimbursed at **100%**.

### Telecommunication costs (during repatriation)

This topic defines the budget allowed for necessary phone or data usage during a medical repatriation.

Reimbursement for telecommunication costs during a repatriation event. Coverage is **€ 125** per event.

### Extension of flight ticket

This topic specifies the financial assistance provided when a medical situation requires changing or extending existing travel plans.

Costs for extending a flight ticket in medical situations. The reimbursement is **€ 1,250**.

### Accommodation costs (during repatriation)

This topic sets the limits for extra lodging expenses incurred while undergoing or awaiting repatriation.

Extra accommodation costs in the context of repatriation. Coverage is **€ 100** per day with a maximum of **€ 2,000** per event.

### Transport of mortal remains (Medical costs section)

This topic outlines the coverage for returning a deceased person to the Netherlands, including necessary paperwork.

The coverage within the medical costs section for the transport of the mortal remains to the Netherlands and necessary documentation. This is **100%** covered up to a maximum of **€ 20,000**.

### Burial or cremation abroad (Medical costs section)

This topic details the alternative reimbursement available when choosing local funeral services instead of repatriation.

The costs for a funeral in the country of temporary residence. The reimbursement is equal to what the transport of the mortal remains would have cost, however up to a maximum of **€ 6,250**.

### Search and rescue operations

This topic specifies the per-event coverage limit for operations aimed at finding or rescuing an insured person.

Costs for search and rescue. Coverage is **€ 25,000** per event.

Standard dental coverage for children up to 18 years. All treatments, except aesthetic ones, are covered up to **€ 375** per year. No deductible applies.

This topic specifies the lifetime limit for orthodontic work and special rules for congenital facial conditions.

Coverage for orthodontics for insured persons up to 21 years. A maximum of **€ 2,000** applies for the total duration of the insurance. The correction of a cleft lip, jaw, or palate is reimbursed at **100%**.

## 1.3 Optional Dental Coverage (from 18 years)

This chapter explains the voluntary dental insurance extension available for adults, including its costs and specific exclusions.

### Dental coverage from 18 years

This topic details the percentage-based reimbursement, annual maximums, and the initial 12-month waiting period for major dental work.

Additional coverage for insured persons aged 18 and older. The reimbursement is **85%** of the costs up to a maximum of **€ 1,150** per insured person per year. ## 2 Repatriation of Mortal Remains

This chapter defines the scope of insurance for the return of a deceased insured person to the Netherlands or local burial arrangements.

This section covers the costs of transporting the mortal remains back to the Netherlands.

#### Transport Costs

Covered at 100% up to a maximum of € 20,000, including necessary documentation and handling costs.

## 3 Extra Flight

This chapter covers the financial consequences of an unexpected recall to the Netherlands, defining the coverage and frequency of payouts.

**Scope**
This insurance covers unforeseen extra flight costs related to a recall to the Netherlands. Reimbursement is based on the cost price of a return ticket, up to the insured amount specified by the insured.

**Conditions**
A payout is due a maximum of once per 12-month period per blood relative or relative by marriage.

**Premium**
The premium is 5% of the specified insured amount per year.

## 4 SOS Assistance

This chapter details the emergency services provided, including search and rescue, repatriation logistics, and zone-based premium structures.

**Coverage Overview**
This topic summarizes the specific benefits for transport, communication, and funeral-related costs under the SOS banner.

- **Repatriation:** 100% coverage for flight costs (scheduled or ambulance plane), including complications during pregnancy.
- **Search and Rescue:** Covered up to € 25,000 per event.
- **Telecommunication:** € 125 per event.
- **Additional Costs:** Ticket extension (€ 1,250) and accommodation (€ 100/day, max € 2,000).
- **Mortal Remains:** Transport to the Netherlands is covered 100% (max € 20,000); local burial is covered up to the transport cost equivalent (max € 6,250).

**Premiums and Zones**
This topic explains how the premium is calculated based on geographic risk (Zone A vs. Zone B).

Premiums vary by zone: Zone A (World excl. Africa) and Zone B (World incl. Africa).

#### Zone B (Incl. Africa)

€ 159 for the insured; € 131 for each subsequent insured.

#### Zone A (Excl. Africa)

€ 176 for the insured; € 148 for each subsequent insured.

#### Note

This section is automatically co-insured with the health insurance section.

## 5 Work Incapacity

This chapter discusses the long-term disability protection for mission workers, outlining payout thresholds and mental health limitations.

### 5.1 / 5.2 / 5.3 / 5.4 Coverage Details

This topic defines the structure of the disability insurance, including choices between constant or rising payouts and the 365-day waiting period.

**Target Group & Structure**
Designed for mission workers requiring financial support during disability.

- **Options:** Choice between constant premium/payout or 3% rising premium/payout.
- **Threshold:** Payouts begin from 45% disability.
- **Waiting Period:** A 365-day own-risk term applies.
- **Mental Conditions:** Payouts for disability due to psychological conditions end after a maximum of 48 months (for policies starting on/after Jan 1, 2018).

### 5.5 / 5.6 / 5.7 Terms and Premiums

This topic details the assessment methods used for disability and provides examples of annual premiums based on age and age limits.

**Duration & Assessment**

- **End Age:** Coverage can run until age 65 or 67.
- **Assessment:** Based on occupational disability; after 2 years, based on suitable labor.

**Annual Premiums (per € 1,000 annual rent)**
Premiums depend on age and the chosen plan (constant vs. rising).

#### Example (Age 30+)

- 65 years (constant): € 45.8.
- 65 years (3% rising): € 51.
- 67 years (constant): € 52.9.
- 67 years (3% rising): € 58.3.

## 7 Legal Aid

This chapter explains the two tiers of legal assistance, highlighting the differences between "Basis" and "Excellent" coverage.

**Coverage Tiers**
This topic specifies which types of law (Criminal, Contract, Labor, Family) are covered and the financial limits for external legal costs.

- **Recourse & Criminal Law:** Unlimited coverage for internal assistance (SRK experts) in both tiers; external costs covered up to € 12,500 (Recourse) or € 25,000 (Criminal).
- **Contract Law (EU):** Unlimited internal assistance; external costs up to € 25,000.
- **Labor & Family Law:** Covered only under the **Excellent** tier (Unlimited internal; External costs € 12,500 for Labor/Family).
- **Minimum Interest:** Disputes must involve a financial interest of at least € 450.

**Annual Premiums**
This topic outlines the costs for individuals and families for each legal aid tier.

- **Single:** € 65 (Basis) / € 170 (Excellent).
- **Family (no children):** € 118 (Basis) / € 195 (Excellent).
- **Family (with children <21):** € 128 (Basis) / € 211 (Excellent).

## 8 Household Contents

This chapter outlines the protection for personal property in the home, including sub-limits for electronics and restrictions based on building material.

**Availability**
Not available for the Caribbean region.

### 8.1 Coverage Overview

This topic lists the insured events such as fire and theft, and provides limits for specific high-value categories.

**Insured Events**
Covers fire, explosion, lightning, theft (after break-in), vandalism, storm, water, and smoke.

- **Sub-limits:** Max € 2,500 per event applies to Audio/Video/Computer equipment and Jewelry.
- **Accommodation:** Temporary stay costs covered up to 10% of the insured amount.

**Premium**
The annual premium is € 4.50 per € 1,000 insured amount.

### 8.2 / 8.3 / 8.4 Restrictions and Discounts

This topic details the exclusions for wooden objects and describes potential discounts for secure or stone buildings.

**Conditions**

- **Wooden Objects:** Never covered.
- **Extra Coverage:** Amounts above € 2,500 for jewelry or electronics require an extra premium of 15% on the excess. Values above € 5,000 require appraisals or invoices.

**Discounts**

- **Stone building/hard roofing:** 10% discount.
- **Electronic security:** 10% discount.
- **Professional guarding:** 5% discount.

## 14 Other

This chapter covers miscellaneous but critical details such as age limits and the application of insurance tax.

### 14.1 Age Limits

This topic specifies when coverage ends for primary insured persons and children.

- **General:** Insurance runs until the insured turns 72.
- **Disability:** Runs until age 65 or 67.
- **Children:** Co-insured until age 21 or marriage. Afterwards, they can start their own policy without medical questions.

### 14.2 Insurance Tax

This topic identifies the specific policy components that are subject to government insurance tax.

Tax applies to Extra Flight, Legal Aid, Household Contents, Instruments, and Private Liability components.
