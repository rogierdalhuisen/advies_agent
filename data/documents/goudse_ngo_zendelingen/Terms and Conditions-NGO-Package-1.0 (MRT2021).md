### 1.20 Terrorism and malicious contamination

This topic outlines the role of the Netherlands Reinsurance Company for Losses from Terrorism (NHT) and the resulting limitations on cover.

It has become clear in the New York attack on 11 September 2001 that terrorism may have more wide-ranging and intrusive effects than was previously thought. For that reason, Dutch insurance companies have set up the Netherlands Reinsurance Company for Losses from Terrorism (NHT). In this umbrella organisation the insurance companies provide cover for the consequences of terrorism.

#### NHT decision is binding

Should you have any loss or damage related to terrorism, please inform us as soon as possible. We will submit a claim for compensation of your damage or loss at the NHT. The NHT will decide whether an event in which loss or damage occurred falls within the scope of terrorism risk. The decision of the NHT is binding for you and for us.

#### Limited cover

If the event in which your loss occurred falls within the scope of terrorism risk, the cover is limited. This limitation applies to any insurance stated on your policy schedule. The cover is limited to the amount paid to us by the NHT. The Clauses Sheet Terrorism Cover at the NHT and the Claims Settlement Protocol define the amount paid to us for compensation by the NHT. If the NHT fully compensates our claim, we are able to reimburse your loss in full.

#### Extent of the cover

You can report your loss up to two years after the decision of the NHT. Then the entitlement to cover will expire.

#### More information?

A detailed description of the term terrorism risk can be found in the Clauses Sheet Terrorism Cover at the NHT. The limitation of the cover is thoroughly explained in the Clauses Sheet. The full text of the Clauses Sheet and the Claims Settlement Protocol can be found at: www.terrorismeverzekerd.nl.

- **Clinical specialist treatment:** The fee of a medical specialist for treatment during hospital admittance; Additional costs that have to be made during clinical treatment.
- **Outpatient specialist treatment:** The fee of the treatment by a medical specialist consulted upon referral by a general practitioner or a dentist for treatment, without admittance to a hospital. Additional costs that occur during outpatient treatment; Costs for outpatient treatment also includes costs for laboratory examination upon referral of the general practitioner, in so far as the hospital or the laboratory charges the costs.

### 2.3 Description of cover

This topic outlines specific medical reimbursements for birth, prescription drugs, therapy, and hospital admissions.

#### 2.3.1 Alternative care

There is a reimbursement for consultations/treatments by an alternative healthcare provider up to a maximum per insured per policy year according to the statement of premiums and cover.

#### 2.3.3 Birth

All costs related to the delivery, up to the maximum as stated in the premiums and cover. During the first policy year a lower maximum amount for reimbursement applies, also mentioned on the statement of premiums and cover.

- **Delivery in a hospital or maternity ward on medical indication:** There is a reimbursement of the nursing costs and additional costs for both mother and child and the fees of the obstetrician.
- **Delivery in a hospital or maternity ward without medical indication:** There is a reimbursement of the nursing costs and additional costs for both mother and child for a maximum of ten days from the date of the delivery to a maximum sum per day in accordance with the statement of premiums and cover, as well as the fee for the obstetrician. In countries where home delivery is not possible, there is a right to reimbursement of 100% of the nursing costs and additional costs for both mother and child for a maximum of ten days, from the date of the delivery.
- **Outpatient delivery or home delivery:** Reimbursement of the obstetrician fees and the costs to use the outpatient clinic.
- **Maternity care:** A maximum reimbursement as stated in the statement of premiums and cover. If the birth takes place in a hospital or a maternity centre (other than an outpatient delivery) a fixed amount will be deducted for every day admitted to the hospital as stated in the statement of cover.

#### 2.3.4 Prescription drugs

We will reimburse the costs of prescription drugs that are included in the most recent version of the Medication Allowance System (Geneesmiddelenvergoedingssysteem).

#### 2.3.14 Medical devices

Costs to purchase, correct, replace, change or repair medical devices are reimbursed according to the Medical Devices Rules.

#### 2.3.21 Hospital

- **Admission:** In case of admission to a hospital and after contact with the emergency centre, reimbursement is made for the nursing costs, the treatment of the medical specialist and any additional costs. Costs for the nursing and the treatment will be limited to a maximum of 365 days per illness.
- **Psychiatric care in a psychiatric institution:** We reimburse the costs for admission to a psychiatric institution for a maximum of 365 days.
