# International Expat Insurance

The "International Expat Insurance" is an international expat insurance package from Henner, offering essential global coverage for expats and their family members. It provides professional assistance worldwide for illness, accident, death, and loss of income. Henner is an internationally recognized administration office for expat insurance, working on behalf of Allianz for this package. Both Henner and Allianz have extensive experience with medical insurance for international organizations, multinational companies, their international employees, and individual expats.

## 1. Introduction

### Start and End Ages

*   **Start Age:** This insurance package can be applied for up to and including age 60.
*   **Medical Expenses Insurance:** No maximum end age.
*   **Life Cover:** Runs up to a maximum age of 60.
*   **Accident Insurance (ADD) and Disability Insurance (TI + PD):** Run up to a maximum age of 66.

### Regions and Nationalities

*   **Eligibility:** Open to all nationalities.
*   **Medical Expenses Insurance (with optional Dental):** Can be applied for regardless of your permanent or temporary country of residence, except if you are a resident of the USA (coverage for emergencies during temporary stays in the USA is included).
*   **Other Insurances (Disability, Life, Accident):** Can only be applied for with a policy address in specific countries: Belgium, Denmark, Germany, Finland, France, Ireland, Italy, Luxembourg, Netherlands, Norway, Austria, Portugal, Spain, or Sweden. You may reside elsewhere, but the policy address must be in one of these listed countries.
*   **Coverage in Risk Areas:** Medical expense coverage continues in countries with orange or red travel advisories.

### General Information

*   **Core Coverage:** Medical Expenses & Medical Evacuation (International Medical and Assistance Insurance) forms the basis of the policy.
*   **Optional Coverages:** Dental, Accident, Life Cover, Disability.
*   **Room Type Coverage:** Private clinics, 2-person rooms (Essential and Bronze) or 1-person room (Gold).
*   **Adventure Sports:** If you participate in "adventure" sports, discuss this in advance to confirm coverage. Excluded sports include rugby, aerial sports (including kitesurfing), (semi)professional sports, winter sports competitions and races, motor races (on land and sea), and rafting. Refer to the policy terms for the complete list and exact descriptions.
*   **Other Insurances:** Insurances not included in this package (e.g., liability, legal assistance, household contents, travel baggage) can be easily arranged separately through other insurers.

## 2. Coverage

For a detailed overview, refer to the **Benefits overview of the International Expat Insurance Package (PDF)**.

### Medical Expenses & Medical Evacuation (International Medical and Assistance Insurance)

*   **Three Coverage Options:** Essential, Bronze, Gold. Differences are detailed in the Benefits overview.
*   **Essential Coverage:** Only covers hospitalizations (inpatient coverage) and medical evacuation.
*   **Bronze and Gold Coverage:** Also cover outpatient treatments.
*   **Medical Cost Limits:** Up to €500,000 (Essential), €1,000,000 (Bronze), or €3,000,000 (Gold) per person per year.
*   **Policy Consistency:** Only one coverage variant and, if chosen, one deductible can apply per policy. It is not possible to choose Essential for yourself and Bronze for your children on the same policy; everyone must choose either Essential or Bronze.
*   **Provider Choice:** Free choice of healthcare providers, coverage in both private and state clinics, worldwide network of hospitals and over 50,000 healthcare providers.
*   **Coverage Area:** Worldwide (temporary coverage for emergencies only in the USA).
*   **Service Team:** Multilingual service team available 24/7.
*   **Direct Billing:** Henner directly pays invoices for clinical treatments to healthcare providers.
*   **Pregnancy/Childbirth Waiting Period (Bronze and Gold):** A 10-month waiting period applies from the policy start date during which Henner does not reimburse costs related to pregnancy or childbirth.
*   **Pregnancy/Childbirth Exclusion (Essential):** All costs for pregnancy and childbirth are excluded under Essential coverage.
*   **Room Type:** Gold coverage includes single-person rooms; Essential and Bronze cover two-person rooms.
*   **Planned Treatments:** Planned medical treatments (non-emergency) must be pre-approved by Henner via a 'prior-agreement procedure'.
*   **War and Civil Unrest Coverage:** Included for medical expenses.

#### Explanation of Medical Evacuation & Repatriation Coverage

*   Medical emergency evacuation and repatriation.
*   Shipment of medication.
*   24/7 medical assistance alarm center.
*   Limited coverage for return costs due to serious family circumstances (1st and 2nd degree relatives). Additional insurance may be advisable depending on your family situation.

#### Explanation of Pregnancy and Childbirth Coverage

*   Henner offers pregnancy and childbirth coverage in two of its medical expense options (Bronze and Gold).
*   **Essential Coverage:** All costs for pregnancy and childbirth are excluded.
*   **Bronze and Gold Coverage:** A 10-month waiting period applies from the policy start date for costs related to pregnancy or childbirth.
*   **Caesarean Section:** Covered only if medically indicated.
*   **Regular Birth:** Covered as an 'outpatient' treatment up to maximum amounts. For Bronze, a 20% co-payment applies to regular birth costs. For births with complications, costs are covered under 'inpatient' coverage up to the annual maximum (at least €1 million), with no co-payment.
*   **Changes for Essential Coverage (Effective January 1, 2025):** The medical coverage of up to €100,000 for babies in the first 3 months after birth will be removed from Essential coverage. Adding a baby to the policy after birth requires a medical acceptance procedure. If accepted, the baby is enrolled from the date of acceptance by Henner.

#### Explanation of USA Coverage

*   **Temporary Stay in USA:** You are insured for unforeseen, urgent medical treatments due to illness and accident, excluding: "Expenses related to pregnancy (and complications thereof) and/or childbirth will not be considered to be Accident or emergency expenses, and will therefore not be covered." Planned treatments in the USA are not covered. Annual coverage is limited to 90 days of stay in the USA. If you expect to be in the USA for more than 90 days per year, contact JoHo Insurances.
*   **Residency in USA:** This insurance cannot be purchased or maintained if you live and work in the USA. Alternatives are available.

#### Explanation of Free Choice, Private Clinics, and Access to Care

*   Henner offers free choice of healthcare providers and access to private clinics.
*   **Prior Agreement:** For foreseen hospital admissions, you must contact Henner in advance for a 'prior-agreement' by completing and emailing a form. This allows Henner to assess the necessity of the treatment, check if the provider is in their network (for direct payment), and evaluate the provider's suitability.
*   **Emergency Care:** For emergency assistance and rapid hospital admission, the hospital will contact Henner at a later time, not in advance.

### Dental Coverage (Dental Plan)

*   **Coverage Options:** Basic (max. €3,000) or Comprehensive (max. €5,000).
*   **Policy Requirement:** Dental coverage must be taken for all insured individuals on the policy.
*   **Waiting Period:** A 1-year waiting period applies for major dental work (e.g., crowns, bridges, implants, oral surgery) from the policy start date.

### Temporary and Permanent Disability (Temporary Incapacity & Permanent Disability)

*   **Coverage:** Loss of income due to illness or accident (excluding disability due to psychological causes).
*   **Temporary Incapacity (TI):** Benefits paid for a maximum of 2 years after the onset of disability. A 90-day waiting period applies before benefits commence.
*   **Permanent Disability (PD):** Benefits paid up to a maximum age of 65. This benefit is only paid if permanent disability is established.
*   **Combination Requirement:** Permanent Disability can only be purchased in combination with Temporary Incapacity.
*   **Self-Employed:** Starting entrepreneurs must demonstrate a regular gross income of at least €2,500 over a minimum period of 6 months prior to application.
*   **Insured Income (Disability Insurance):** Maximum 80% of fixed annual income, ranging from a minimum of €12,000 to a maximum of €120,000 annually.

### Life Cover (Life Cover)

*   **Benefit:** Payout to beneficiaries upon death.
*   **Coverage Limits:** Minimum €50,000, maximum €500,000.
*   **Age Limit:** Can be maintained up to and including age 60.
*   **Premium:** Age-dependent.

### Accidental Death and Invalidity (Accidental Death and Invalidity)

*   **Benefit (Accidental Death):** Payout upon death due to an accident.
*   **Benefit (Permanent Invalidity):** Payout for permanent invalidity due to an accident.
*   **Coverage Limits:** Minimum €50,000, maximum €500,000.
*   **Eligibility:** Only available for adults.
*   **Age Limit:** Can be maintained up to and including age 65.

## 3. Premiums

*   **Factors:** Premiums depend on the coverage level, age, and deductible.
*   For a detailed overview, refer to the **Premiums overview of the International Expat Insurance Package (PDF)**.
*   **Surcharge:** Based on the health declaration, the insurer may decide to apply a premium surcharge.
*   **Annual Adjustment:** Premiums are adjusted annually on January 1st based on age and current rates.
*   **Payment Terms:** No surcharge for semi-annual or quarterly payments. Monthly payments are not possible.

## 4. How to Apply

### Step 1: Complete the Application Form

*   Fill out the **Application Form & Health Declaration (PDF)** completely and legibly.
*   The application form includes filling instructions.
*   Scan the fully completed and signed application form.
*   *The application form includes a health declaration where you must answer several health questions. Based on these answers, the insurer will assess whether to insure you and under what conditions. Exclusions for certain conditions may apply, as well as adjustments to the premium and/or deductible.*

### Step 2: Email the Required Documents to JoHo Insurances

1.  Completed application form.
2.  Copy of the applicant's (policyholder's) passport.
3.  Send to: contact@johoinsurances.org

**Applying for Disability and/or Life Cover?**
The provided Disability/Life quote will specify any additional information and documents required.

1.  Proof of income (e.g., payslip, contract).
2.  JoHo Insurance Disability/Life closing document.

If you have not yet received a quote and wish to apply for Disability and/or Life cover, please contact us.

### Step 3: Application Receipt

*   We will confirm receipt of your application within 2 working days.
*   Once complete, we will ensure the application reaches the insurer.

#### Contract Term, Changes During Policy Period, and End Age

*   **Contract, Premium, Deductible, and Invoice Term:** Runs from January 1st to December 31st.
*   **Start Date:** You can start the insurance on any desired day (not retroactively).
*   **Policy Changes:** Changes to your insurance (coverage, deductible, etc.) can be made once a year and will, after acceptance by Henner, take effect on January 1st of the following year.
*   **Cancellation:** After the minimum term of 12 months, the insurance can be canceled on the 1st day of any upcoming month with one month's notice.

#### End Age

*   **Medical Expenses Insurance:** No maximum end age.
*   **Life Cover:** Runs up to a maximum age of 60 (the year in which the insured is 60 years old on January 1st, this insurance stops). For this reason, applications for this insurance can only be made up to and including age 59.
*   **Accident Insurance (ADD) and Disability Insurance (TI + PD):** Run up to a maximum age of 66 (the year in which the insured is 66 years old on January 1st, these insurances stop).

## 5. Services After Policy Activation

**Emergency Medical Assistance**
In medical emergencies, you can contact Henner's alarm center 24/7 worldwide for immediate assistance.

**Personal Online Environment & MyHenner App**
After your Henner insurance is accepted, you will receive login details for your personal online environment. Here you will find all your coverage information, documents, and can easily declare your medical expenses. This information is also available in the MyHenner app.

**Claiming Medical Expenses**

*   **Via your online environment:** Use the e-claim option in your personal online environment. Instructions are also available on the website.
*   **Via the MyHenner app.**

Always keep original invoices in case Henner has additional questions about your claim.

**Policy Cancellation**

*   After the first year, you can cancel the insurance daily with a one-month notice period.
*   Interim cancellation within the first year is only possible if you are mandatorily insured through a national system in your country of residence, or upon returning to the Netherlands.
