## PREVENTION IS BETTER

### Know where you're going

Traveling is wonderful, but there are also risks involved. You are insured for some of these with this Special ISIS insurance. But of course it's better to prevent unpleasant situations.

For example, it's always wise to get the right travel vaccinations; you can find more about this on ggdreisvaccinaties.nl. And familiarize yourself with your destination beforehand. The government provides travel advice for the entire world (see nederlandwereldwijd.nl). Only when a country is 'green' are there no specific safety risks.

Always read the travel advice for your destination before you leave and take into account the safety risks mentioned in it. For example, avoid crowded places, travel during daylight and be aware of local customs and norms. Also pay attention to current health risks, such as epidemics, and realize what impact it can have if you need medical help.

### Watch your belongings

If you take baggage on a trip it's important that you take good care of it. In case of insufficient care to prevent loss, theft or damage to your belongings, you cannot claim on this insurance. You can read more about this in article 10 under 'Insufficient care'.

We want you to be able to enjoy your vacation. The following tips can help you with this.

- Take as few valuable items and cash as possible
- Don't take subscriptions season tickets and the like if you won't use them during your trip
- Always carry money cards and travel documents in a 'concealed' manner in a chest or belly bag and keep as much physical contact as possible with cameras and bags
- Write down phone numbers of the alarm center and numbers of your passport or ID card driving and registration certificate travel and health insurance bank card and credit card Always keep this information separate from the original documents and also save them online

#### Are you traveling independently by car (or other vehicle)?

- Only pack on the day of departure
- During rest stops park in a place where you can keep sight of your car
- Don't leave valuable items money and important documents behind if you don't have sight of your car Also make sure your other baggage is not visible from outside
- Always lock your car and caravan properly even during short rest stops or when refueling
- Take your baggage to your overnight location when staying overnight en route
- Unload your car on the day of arrival

#### Are you traveling by plane, bus, train or boat?

- Take valuable items, money, important documents, fragile items and medicines in your hand luggage.

#### Are you at your destination?

- Don't leave your valuable items, money and important documents unattended in your hotel room, tent, caravan, car, on the beach or anywhere.
- Use lockers if they are available. You can also always carry your belongings with you.

#### Does something go wrong anyway? And is this not caused by the transport company?

Then immediately file a report with the police in case of theft, disappearance or loss of baggage, travel documents and/or money and request a copy of the official report.

Is your baggage damaged or missing during transport by a transport company? Then a police report is not necessary. However, in case of loss or damage to checked baggage on flights, you must always have a PIR (Property Irregularity Report) drawn up. Are you traveling by another means of transport? Then have a written statement drawn up by the transport company.

### ARTICLE 5- VALIDITY PERIOD

#### Extension

The coverage starts at the moment you leave your home for a trip abroad and ends at the moment you are back home. Depending on your age, coverage for medical costs and the chosen insured region, there may be limitations on the term of the insurance. The main rule is that the Special ISIS insurance can run until the age of 65.

There are 2 exceptions:

1.  Insurance with Worldwide coverage incl. the US can be maintained until the age of 45.
2.  Insurance with coverage Europe or World excl. the US can be maintained in the 'All-in' variant for medical costs until the age of 55.

#### Extension

You can take out the insurance for a maximum of one year. If you stay abroad longer, you can extend the insurance. The total continuous insurance period is maximum four years. Extension of your insurance is only possible through your ISIS insurance advisor.

#### Please note!

You must extend on time, so before the end date stated on your policy schedule. Are you too late? Then the application for extension is assessed in the same way as a new application for insurance is assessed. This also means that you are not insured for damage caused by, or resulting from, events that took place before the extension date.

Does a new Special ISIS insurance start within three months after the previous one has ended? Then claims relating to the previous insured period for dental costs or for a phone continue to count when determining the maximum benefit for these coverages. All insured periods count towards determining the maximum term of four years.

#### Back later through no fault of your own?

Can you not be back in the Netherlands on the end date of the insurance due to delay, illness or an accident? Then the insurance remains valid until the first possible time when you can be back in the Netherlands. But this extended coverage never lasts longer than thirty days. You do not have to pay an additional premium for this.

### ARTICLE 7- GENERAL EXCLUSIONS

#### We do not compensate damage that occurred:

- because you commit a crime;
- through the possession or use of weapons by you;
- through your intent or conscious recklessness;
- through participation in fights or quarrels;
- in connection with the use of alcohol or drugs;
- through engaging in dangerous sports;

- through nuclear reactions (see explanation below).

#### Explanation nuclear reactions

- **No coverage:** We do not compensate damage caused by, occurred during or resulting from nuclear reactions. Regardless of how these occurred.
- **Coverage:** We do compensate damage caused by radioactive nuclides. These nuclides must then be located outside a nuclear installation and be used or intended for industrial, commercial, agricultural, medical, scientific, educational or (non-military) security purposes. For this, the relevant authority must have received a government permit, insofar as this is required. This permit must relate to the development, use, storage and disposal of radioactive materials. This coverage does not apply if according to a law or treaty a third party is liable for the damage suffered.

_Nuclear installation means a nuclear installation within the meaning of the Nuclear Accidents Liability Act (Staatsblad 1979-225) as well as a nuclear installation on board a ship._

### ARTICLE 8- HOW ARE YOU INSURED IN CASE OF TERRORISM?

The attack in New York on September 11, 2001, made it clear that terrorism can have much greater and more drastic consequences than was assumed up to that time. Insurers in the Netherlands have therefore established the Dutch Reinsurance Company for Terrorism Damage (NHT). In it they collectively house coverage of the consequences of terrorism.

#### NHT makes binding decision

Do you have damage related to terrorism, report this to us as soon as possible. We will then submit a claim for your damage to the NHT. The NHT determines whether the event in which your damage occurred falls under the terrorism risk. The decision of the NHT is binding for you and for us.

---

#### Limited coverage

The coverage is limited if it has been established that the event in which your damage occurred falls under the terrorism risk. This limitation applies to each separate component of your insurance. The coverage is limited to the amount that the NHT pays out to us. The 'Clause sheet terrorism coverage at the NHT' and the 'Claims settlement protocol' regulate which amount the NHT pays out to us. If the NHT pays out our claim in full, we can also fully compensate your damage.

#### Expiry of coverage

You can report your damage to us up to two years after the NHT has made its decision. After that, any right to coverage expires.

#### Want to know more?

A detailed description of the concept of terrorism risk can be found in the 'Clause sheet terrorism coverage at the NHT'. You can also read exactly how the limitation of coverage works there. The full text of this clause sheet and the 'Claims settlement protocol' can be found at: terrorismeverzekerd.nl.

## THE COMPONENTS - BAGGAGE

### ARTICLE 9-YOUR BAGGAGE COVERAGE

We compensate all damage to your baggage and personal belongings if the damage occurred due to external misfortune. By this we mean loss, theft, damage and disappearance, among other things.

#### Phone

For phones there is a limitation. Damage to a phone is only compensated if you have the Super or Super+ coverage (see your policy schedule) and the phone is stolen or damaged. Loss and disappearance of the phone are not insured. The maximum compensation for phones (€200,- with the Super coverage and € 400,- with the Super+ coverage) applies for the total duration of the insurance including extensions and therefore not per event.

#### Sent ahead or forwarded baggage

Is baggage sent ahead or forwarded by plane, boat, bus or train? And does this shipment take place during the insured period? Then there is coverage for damage to the baggage that occurred during this transport, except when it concerns valuable items. There is only coverage if you can show a shipping receipt.

#### Replacement clothing/toiletries

Can you not access your baggage 24 hours after arrival at your holiday destination? Then we compensate the necessary purchase of clothing and toiletries up to a maximum of € 125,- (Standard and Super coverage) or € 150,- (Super+ coverage). No deductible applies.

#### Police report

In case of theft or disappearance of baggage you must always file a report with the police in the place where the incident occurred and have a police report drawn up. If the police charge costs for this, we compensate these up to a maximum of € 50,- per event. Did the theft or disappearance occur while the item was in a baggage compartment of for example a train, bus, plane or boat? Then do not file a report with the police but with the transport company and have them draw up a report. Always ask for a copy of the report.

#### Damage to hotel or other location where you stay overnight

Damage to the hotel or other location where you stay overnight, and for which you are liable, is compensated up to the maximum amount stated in the coverage overview. We will then deduct the deductible of € 25,- per event from the compensation.

### ARTICLE 10 - EXCLUSIONS BAGGAGE

The Baggage component has extensive coverage. But we do not compensate everything. In addition to the exclusions of article 7, there are the following exclusions for this component.

#### Deductible

We do not compensate the first € 75,- (Standard coverage) or € 50,- (Super coverage) damage per event. The Super+ coverage has no deductible. On your policy schedule you can see which coverage you have chosen.

#### Insufficient care

You do not receive compensation if you do not take good care of your belongings or are careless with them. You must do everything to prevent and reduce damage. You store your belongings and especially your important documents and valuable items in the safest place available at that time, for example in a safe.

By safest place we mean for example that you:

- only leave important documents and valuable items under supervision;
- take important documents and valuable items and medicines as hand luggage during travel by plane, boat, train or bus;
- do not leave important documents and valuable items in a vehicle, so not out of sight and not in a closed trunk either;
- put your other baggage (no valuable items and papers) out of sight in a vehicle in a locked trunk. A roof box must also be properly locked;
- take important documents and valuable items and medicines as hand luggage during travel by plane, boat, train or bus.

---

#### Money

Loss or theft of money is not compensated.

#### Misuse of bank card or credit card

We do not compensate damage due to misuse of a bank card or credit card.

#### Subscriptions and credits

We do not compensate subscriptions, season tickets, passes, admission tickets and the like that you have taken with you but that are not needed during the trip. We also do not compensate the value of credit vouchers, (phone) subscriptions and phone credits.

#### Specific objects and items

We do not compensate the following objects and items:

- phones and accessories if you have the Standard coverage (see your policy schedule);
- collections, antiques and art;
- medical instruments;
- tools, car and camper accessories;
- built-in navigation systems;
- folding trailers, caravans, watercraft, vehicles, riding or aircraft and associated accessories.

#### We also do not compensate damage to baggage that occurred:

- through wear and tear and other gradually acting (weather) influences;
- through normal use, such as stains, scratches, scrapes or dents on suitcases and (travel) bags if these can still be used; But for example also damage to a drone occurred during its use.
- through predators or rodents, insects and vermin;
- through an inherent defect of the item. This is a poor property of the item itself, which was not caused by an event from outside;
- through theft from a properly locked space without signs of break-in;
- as an indirect consequence of an insured event (consequential damage). If your key is stolen, for example, you do receive compensation for that key (direct consequence) but not for new locks (consequential damage).

### ARTICLE 11- WHAT APPLIES IF YOU HAVE DAMAGE TO YOUR BAGGAGE?

#### We compensate:

- never more than the purchase value;
- the purchase value of items that are not older than one year and of which you can show proof of purchase;
- the current value of items that are older than one year and of which you can show proof of purchase;
- the repair costs up to a maximum of the current value of damaged items that can be repaired.

#### What if your items are recovered?

Are your items recovered within three months and have you not yet used the compensation to replace the items? Then you are obliged to buy them back for an amount equal to the paid claim amount.

## MEDICAL COSTS (OPTIONAL)

#### MEDICAL COSTS

If you co-insure medical costs, you can choose between the All-in Option and the Extra Option. Below you can read more about both options. Choosing the right option is your own responsibility.

If you have the World including US coverage, a maximum age of 44 years applies. As soon as you turn 45, coverage stops.

With this option you must first claim medical costs from your Dutch health insurance. You are therefore also obliged to have Dutch health insurance, as prescribed in the Health Insurance Act. Does your health insurer not compensate (part of) the costs because you have chosen a higher deductible than the mandatory deductible? Then we do not compensate (this part of) these costs either.

Please note: sometimes you can no longer continue your 'regular' health insurance. This can happen if:

- you receive income abroad from a 'side job or an internship allowance;
- you deregister yourself at the municipality in the Netherlands

You are then also no longer insured with us for medical costs. Contact your ISIS insurance advisor quickly. They can convert your insurance to the All-in option (see above). If you are already under treatment at the time of conversion to All-in, article 13 applies.

We only compensate the costs if the treatment, purchase of medicines, etc. cannot be postponed until you are back in the Netherlands.

These are the costs of:

- visit to doctor or specialist;

- medicines and bandages prescribed by the doctor;

- first prostheses that you need after an accident and that a doctor prescribes;
- the rental of aids abroad, such as crutches or a walker, that you need after an accident and that a doctor prescribes, up to a maximum of € 100,-;
- complications in the first twelve weeks of a pregnancy.

When you have returned to the Netherlands, our compensation stops, with the exception of the follow-up treatment costs as mentioned below.

#### Accident

Sudden and unexpected violence acting on your body from outside. This violence must have caused you to suffer bodily injury directly and in one instant. This injury must be medically determinable.

The following is also understood as an accident:
a. **Acute poisoning:** You are acutely poisoned if you suddenly and unintentionally ingested gases, vapors, liquid or solid substances. Are you poisoned by the use of medicines or because you ingested substances that caused an allergic reaction? Then we do not provide a benefit.
b. **Infection by disease germs or an allergic reaction:** Your infection must have arisen as a direct result of an involuntary fall by you into water or another substance. Or because you entered the water or substance to save people, animals or property.
c/d. **Suffocation, drowning, freezing, sunstroke and heatstroke.** The unintentional and sudden ingestion of substances or objects into the digestive tract, airways, eyes or ears causing internal injury. The penetration of disease germs or substances that cause an allergic reaction is not included.
e. **Exhaustion, starvation, dehydration and sunburn:** Only if these are the result of unforeseen circumstances.
f. **Wound infection or blood poisoning:** This must have arisen because disease germs penetrated an injury. This only applies if this injury is the result of a covered accident.
g. **Worsening or complications of injury:** This injury must be the result of a covered accident. And the worsening or complications must be a direct result of first aid. Or of medical treatment.

#### Wrongful act

A wrongful act is a concept described in the Civil Code. It is a:

- violation of a right and
- something done or omitted in violation of a legal duty or what is appropriate in social interaction according to unwritten law

#### Intent

There is intent if the damage is intended by the insured or is the certain consequence of their action or omission. It does not matter whether damage occurred to the property of this insured or to the property of other insureds.

#### Personal injury

Damage to a person. There must be injury or impairment of health. Or death. Personal injury also includes all damage resulting from this.
