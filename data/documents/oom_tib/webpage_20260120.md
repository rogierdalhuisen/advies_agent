# OOM Temporary Abroad Insurance

The OOM Temporary Abroad insurance package is designed for Dutch and Belgian nationals seeking temporary insurance coverage while abroad. This package offers coverage for unforeseen events, including health care, travel baggage, cancellation, liability, contents, legal assistance, and accidents.

## 1. Characteristics

The OOM Temporary Abroad insurance package is offered by OOM Verzekeringen, a Dutch insurer with extensive experience in international insurance, particularly for long-term stays abroad and special situations. This package is intended for Dutch and Belgian nationals who wish to be insured abroad for a maximum period of 2 years. It offers various modules that can be purchased separately, with coverage and premiums varying per chosen module.

**Key features of the insurance:**

- **Target Group:** Suitable for various reasons for stay, such as living, working, secondment, retirement, or volunteer work.
- **Residence Insurance:** Focused on coverage and assistance at the place of residence, not primarily on repatriation or medical evacuation.
- **Age:** The maximum application age is 69 years.
- **Application Location:** Applications can be submitted even if the insured is already abroad.
- **Purpose:** The insurance can serve as a bridge until the insured qualifies for national insurance in their new country of residence.
- **Coverage for Risky Sports and Activities:** Coverage for many adventurous and risky activities (sports, work).
- **Duration\*:** The maximum continuous duration is 2 years. Extension is possible, provided the total duration of 2 years is not exceeded.
- **Acceptance Process:** OOM assesses each application based on a health declaration. Pre-existing conditions may be excluded from coverage, and the application may be rejected.

**_\* Explanation of Duration and End Date_**

The OOM Temporary Abroad insurance has a fixed end date that you choose upon purchase. The maximum duration is 2 years.

This insurance always ends on the chosen end date, regardless of your personal situation. For example, if you are hospitalized at that time, coverage stops, and you cannot re-insure. This can lead to high costs for medical care or repatriation at your own expense, as another insurer will not cover these costs. We advise purchasing the insurance directly for a longer or even the maximum period of 2 years. The premium is paid monthly, without surcharge, and you can cancel the insurance daily with a refund for the unused period. This provides flexibility without unnecessary risk. By choosing a longer duration immediately, you avoid this risk.

If there is a high probability that you will need to be insured for a longer period, we recommend an insurance with a longer duration for more certainty.

## 2. Coverage

The OOM Temporary Abroad insurance consists of several components. You can choose which coverages you want to include:

1.  Health Insurance (including SOS coverage)
2.  Dental Insurance
3.  Travel Insurance
4.  Liability Insurance
5.  Legal Assistance Insurance
6.  Accident Insurance
7.  Contents Insurance

### Health Insurance (including SOS coverage)

The health insurance offers three coverage options: Light, Regular, and Comfort. Light is only available for insured persons aged 16 to 49.

- The maximum age at application is 69 years.
- No discount applies if you are required to maintain basic health insurance in the Netherlands.
- In many countries, free **choice of healthcare provider** (both public and private care) applies. In some "expensive" countries with high healthcare costs, free choice of care is not applicable\*.
- Hospital costs are fully reimbursed based on **standard class** in a hospital. Any additional costs for a private or double room are not reimbursed. This helps keep healthcare costs and premiums manageable.
- You can choose from the following **deductibles** per person per year: €500, €1,000, €2,000, or €5,000.

#### \* Limited free choice of healthcare provider in "expensive" countries

- In the following countries, **prior authorization** is mandatory before medical treatments (including hospital admission, medical specialist, outpatient clinic, pregnancy, and childbirth): **United States, Singapore, Malaysia, Indonesia, China, Hong Kong, Thailand, Mexico, and the United Arab Emirates.**
- For this, contact the **OOM Emergency Center** at +31 70 353 2135. Clearly state that it is a _pre-authorization request_. After approval, you will receive a payment guarantee and be informed which healthcare provider may perform the treatment. **Note:** Without prior authorization in the mentioned countries, there is **no right to reimbursement**.
- **For acute care**, prior authorization is not required. This also applies to countries other than those mentioned above. Acute care refers to medical assistance that is immediately necessary to prevent serious injury or death. In case of an emergency hospital admission, contact the alarm center as soon as possible.

#### Temporary stay in the aforementioned countries?

- If you are temporarily staying (maximum 3 months) in one of the "expensive" countries, while your country of residence is elsewhere, adjust the regional coverage for your policy (temporarily) via MijnOOM. If you do not, OOM will only partially or not at all reimburse any medical costs (emergency and non-emergency).
- If you stay longer than 3 months in one of these countries, you must submit a new application. A medical acceptance procedure will apply again.

### Dental Insurance

Dental coverage reimburses dental costs and can only be purchased in combination with health insurance.

- Children up to 3 years old are covered free of charge.
- From 4 years old, a premium is charged.

### Travel Insurance

The travel insurance offers **continuous coverage** as long as you stay outside the Netherlands.

- Reimburses transportation costs for **emergency return to the Netherlands** in life-threatening situations or death of family (1st and 2nd degree).
- **Baggage coverage** only applies to personal belongings. Business items, such as employer's phones or laptops, are not covered.
- The maximum reimbursement for baggage applies **per year**, not per trip.
- Coverage only applies during stays **outside the permanent residence**.

**Note:** Medical costs and repatriation are **not covered** by travel baggage insurance. These are exclusively insured through health insurance.

### Cancellation Insurance

Preferably purchase this coverage **within 7 days of booking**. After this period, there is no coverage for circumstances already known at the time of booking.

For short trips, we advise the coverage **to start on the day of booking** (not on the departure date). The greatest risk of cancellation is before departure.

### Liability Insurance

The liability insurance covers damage you cause as a **private individual**.

- Damage incurred while performing work is **not covered**.
- Damage to a **rented property** is excluded. There are only limited other insurances that cover this.
- However, there is coverage for liability related to a **vacant or uninhabited owned home in the Netherlands**, provided it is not exclusively intended for rental.
- Damage caused by a **motor vehicle** (car, scooter, etc.) is excluded.

### Legal Assistance Insurance

This insurance offers legal assistance _in kind_: in case of a dispute, you will be assigned a lawyer. Not all legal issues are covered; therefore, read the conditions carefully.

- Some countries may be excluded.
- Employment disputes are only insured under the **Excellent coverage** (available for insured persons in Europe or the USA).
- Legal assistance for your own (rented) home in the Netherlands can be additionally insured.

### Accident Insurance

You can choose coverage for:

- only permanent disability,
- only death,
- or both.

### Contents Insurance

This coverage insures the contents in your **permanent home abroad**.

- (Co-)rented contents are not covered.
- **Limitations apply to valuables** – check the policy conditions.

## 3. Premium

The premium depends on factors such as age, chosen insurances, coverage, deductible, and region.

The insurance has 5 regions:

- A World
- B+ World except region A United States
- B World except region A, B+ United States, Singapore
- C+ World except region A, B+, B United States, Singapore, China, Hong Kong, Malaysia, Indonesia
- C World except region A, B+, B and C+ United States, Singapore, China, Hong Kong, Malaysia, Indonesia, Thailand, United Arab Emirates, Mexico

Explanation:

- Region C is the lowest region, region A is the highest.
- Choose Region A if you desire coverage in the USA. If you only travel sporadically to the USA, you might consider temporary additional coverage.
- Coverage in a higher region is limited. If you frequently travel to a country in a higher coverage region, discuss the best choice of region.
- Healthcare in the United States: If you are insured for region B+, B, C+, or C, you will not receive reimbursement for medical costs in the United States.
- Healthcare in other higher regions: If you are insured for region B, C+, or C and incur costs in a higher region (except the United States), OOM will only reimburse 50% of the costs, provided they fall under your coverage.

## 4. How to apply for the insurance?

Apply for the OOM Temporary Abroad insurance package via OOM Verzekeringen's online application module.

1.  Application instructions are available (PDF) - Important information and useful tips for applying for the OOM Temporary Abroad insurance package.
2.  If you have **Belgian nationality**, please read the specific instruction document (PDF) carefully before applying.
3.  If you want coverage for medical costs, always apply for health insurance (SOS is included), as the travel insurance in this package does not cover medical costs!

## 5. After applying for the insurance:

### Payment Method:

Automatic debit from SEPA bank account, bank transfer, Mastercard/VISA, or PayPal.

### From what moment in the application process is there coverage?

- As long as the application has not yet been accepted, there is no insurance agreement.
- As long as there is no agreement, medical issues (changes in health situation) must be reported to OOM (Disclosure Duty\*). OOM will include these new circumstances in the assessment.
- Only after an insurance has been explicitly accepted (you receive a policy or have informed OOM that you agree to a proposal):
  - you have certainty that the insurance will start on the agreed\*\* effective date;
  - changes in the health situation no longer need to be reported; from the effective date of the OOM insurance, these medical issues are then also covered.

For this last reason, we recommend submitting an application as early as possible. This can be done up to 2 months before the desired effective date.

_\* Disclosure duty during the application process._

- OOM provides an explanation of the disclosure duty in a special document.
- You must fill in everything truthfully when applying.
- Changes in the situation (until the moment of acceptance) must also be actively reported to OOM.

_\*\* In case of a proposal regarding the medical situation, OOM will not let an insurance start earlier than the date you received the proposal._

**IMPORTANT: Coverage (from the agreed effective date) only begins once there is an agreement with or a policy document from OOM Verzekeringen.**
