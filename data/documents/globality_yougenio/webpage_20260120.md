# Globality Yougenio - Health Insurance

## 1. Introduction

Globality Health is an international health insurer with a special focus on emigrants, retirees, and expats, offering insurance for people living and/or working abroad. Globality Health has over 80 years of experience in health insurance and is part of Munich Re, a large international insurer and reinsurer.

## 2. Coverage and Features

There are 4 different coverage options available:

- **Essential**: Inpatient coverage only.
- **Classic**: Inpatient and partial outpatient coverage, but excludes, for example, pregnancy.
- **Plus**: Extensive inpatient and outpatient coverage, including pregnancy and dental care.
- **Top**: Most comprehensive inpatient and outpatient coverage, including pregnancy and dental care.

For a detailed overview of the coverages, please refer to the Globality Health coverage overview document.

### Features

- Insurance for medical costs and additional expenses like repatriation.
- Individual policy for people of virtually all nationalities.
- No age limit for applying for the insurance.
- Free choice of healthcare provider.
- Various deductibles are possible.
- A waiting period of 10 months applies for pregnancy.
- 5 regions are used. The region where you live partly determines the premium.
- Available for expats, emigrants, and their families.
- "Locals" cannot be co-insured, not even as a partner (co-insured person).

### For which countries can Yougenio be applied?

Yougenio can be applied for in most countries worldwide. However, strict legislation regarding the offering of insurance applies in some countries. Whether it is permitted to apply for Yougenio depends, among other things, on:

- The country of destination.
- The country from which an application is made.

## 3. Premium & Quote

The premium depends on factors such as age, chosen insurance, coverage, deductible, and region. You can view premiums online and request a quote via email.

## 4. How to apply for the insurance online?

You can apply for the insurance entirely online via Globality's online calculation module.

### Important tips for applying and filling out the form!

In the first screen, you will see the following questions, among others:

1.  Nationality
2.  Country of future residence
3.  "Where are you currently located?"

### Point of attention 1: Local?

If your (and/or your partner's) nationality (1) and new country of residence (2) are the same, you are considered a "local." There is a high probability that the insurer will not accept the application. Please contact us before applying so we can check the situation for you before you submit the application.

### Point of attention 2: Place of filling out and signing ("Where are you currently located?")

The location of the (online) application is assessed by the insurer due to laws and regulations. If you fill out the application from the Netherlands or another EU country, there is usually no restriction. If you fill out the application from a country outside the EU, there is a chance that the application will be rejected entirely for "legal reasons." Since you are applying online and our website is registered in NL, you can indicate that you are signing the application in NL.

### Point of attention 3: Sanctioned countries

Globality does not provide coverage in sanctioned countries and is also not allowed to offer insurance for those countries. Which countries are under sanction changes frequently. The online module will notify you if this applies.

### Point of attention 4: Medical report by a doctor from 65 years of age

For individuals aged 65 or older who wish to apply for insurance, Globality requires them to have a medical report prepared by a doctor at their own expense and submit this report to Globality after the online application has been made. The doctor must use the provided medical examination form.
