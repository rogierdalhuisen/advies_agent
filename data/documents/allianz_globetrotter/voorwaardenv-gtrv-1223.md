## Description of Coverages

In this section we describe the different types of coverages included in your insurance. We explain each type of coverage and the specific conditions you must meet to be able to claim the coverage. Keep in mind that exclusions may apply.

### A. SOS Assistance

This coverage is only in effect if stated on your Coverage Overview. It will also state the amount(s) for which you are insured.

**Important:**
Is your emergency acute and life-threatening? Then immediately engage local emergency services. We do not provide medical or emergency services.

- We comply with all national and international laws and regulations, including any travel restrictions in effect. We can only provide our services if we have the required approvals from local authorities.

#### Emergency evacuation (your transport to the nearest suitable medical facility)

If you become seriously ill, suffer serious injury or get a medical condition (this also applies if you contract an epidemic or pandemic disease such as COVID-19) during your trip, and we determine that local medical facilities cannot provide appropriate medical care:

1. Our medical team consults with the local doctor to obtain the necessary information to make the right decisions regarding your overall health condition;
2. We determine what the nearest suitable hospital or other suitable medical facility is. We arrange your transport there and we reimburse the costs of that transport, and
3. We arrange a medical escort. We reimburse the costs if we determine that you need one.

This only applies if:
a. You or someone else on your behalf contacts us and we arrange all transport in advance. If we have not approved or arranged the transport, we will only reimburse up to the amount we would have had to pay if we had arranged the transport. We are not responsible for transport not approved or arranged by us;
b. All decisions about your evacuation are made by medical professionals who are authorized in the countries where they practice their medical profession;
c. You agree with the decisions made by our support and medical teams. If you do not agree, we are no longer responsible or liable for the consequences of your decisions. We may also decide not to provide coverage;
d. One or more emergency transport providers can and will transport you from your current location to the hospital or medical facility.

#### Medical repatriation (your return home after receiving care)

If you become seriously ill, suffer serious injury or get a medical condition (this also applies if you contract an epidemic or pandemic disease such as COVID-19) during your trip, and our medical team together with the treating doctor determines that you are medically stable enough to travel:

1. We arrange and reimburse your transport via regular scheduled services of a regular carrier in the same class as you originally booked for your return trip, unless transport in another class is medically necessary. We reimburse the costs to which you are entitled, minus refunds for unused tickets. You will be transported to one of the following places:
   a. Your home address;
   b. A place of your choice in the country where you live; or
   c. A medical facility near your home address or in a place of your choice in the country where you live. The medical facility must be able and willing to accept you as a patient. And the facility must be approved by our medical team as suitable for your continued care.
2. We arrange a medical escort and reimburse the costs if our medical team determines that you need one.

This only applies if:
a. Special provisions are medically necessary for your transport (for example, if more than one seat is medically necessary for you to travel);
b. You or someone else on your behalf contacts us and we arrange all transport in advance. If we have not approved or arranged the transport, we will only reimburse up to the amount we would have had to pay if we had arranged the transport. We are not responsible for transport not approved or arranged by us;
c. All decisions about your repatriation are made by medical professionals who are authorized in the countries where they practice their medical profession;
d. You agree with the decisions made by our support and medical teams. If you do not agree, we are no longer responsible or liable for the consequences of your decisions. We may also decide not to provide coverage;
e. One or more emergency transport providers can and will transport you from your current location to your chosen destination.

#### Visit by a friend or family member

Does the treating doctor tell you that you must stay in hospital for more than 5 days during your trip, or that your condition is life-threatening? Then we arrange and reimburse a return ticket in economy class from a transport company for one friend or family member to stay with you, and also the accommodation costs up to the maximum amount stated in your Coverage Overview.

This only applies if you or someone else on your behalf contacts us and we arrange all transport in advance. If we have not approved or arranged the transport, we will only reimburse up to the amount we would have had to pay if we had arranged the transport. We are not responsible for transport not approved or arranged by us.

#### Return of dependent persons (ensuring minors and dependent persons can get home)

Do you pass away? Or does the treating doctor tell you that you must stay in hospital for more than 24 hours during your trip? Then we arrange and reimburse the transport of your traveling companions who are under 18 years old or who are dependent on you and need constant supervision and care. We arrange and reimburse transport to one of the following places:

1. Your home address; or
2. A place of your choice in the country where you live.

We arrange and reimburse the transport of an adult family member to accompany your traveling companions who are under 18 years old or who are dependent on you and need constant supervision and care, if we deem this necessary. Transport takes place via a transport company in the same class as you originally booked. We deduct the refunds for unused tickets to which you are entitled from the total amount to be paid.

This only applies if:
a. You are admitted to hospital or if you pass away, and there is no other adult person traveling with you who can care for traveling companions under 18 years old or dependent persons;
b. You or someone else on your behalf contacts us and we arrange all transport in advance. If we have not approved or arranged the transport, we will only reimburse up to the amount we would have had to pay if we had arranged the transport. We are not responsible for transport not approved or arranged by us.

#### Repatriation of your mortal remains

We arrange and reimburse the reasonable and necessary services and supplies for the transport of your mortal remains to one of the following places:

1. A funeral home near your home address, or
2. A funeral home of your choice in the country where you live.

This only applies if:
a. Someone on your behalf contacts us and we arrange all transport in advance. If we have not approved or arranged the transport, we will only reimburse up to the amount we would have had to pay if we had arranged the transport. We are not responsible for transport not approved or arranged by us, and
b. You pass away during your trip.

Does a family member decide to arrange your funeral, burial or cremation at the place of your death? Then we reimburse the necessary costs up to the amount it would have cost us to transport your mortal remains to a funeral home near your home address.

#### Earlier or later return

Do you have to interrupt or cancel your trip or do you have to stay longer due to an insured event listed below? Then we reimburse:

- c. Necessary transport costs you incur to continue your trip or return to your home address. We reimburse either the return ticket from the carrier to your home address or that part of your original return ticket that you do not get refunded, but not both.
- d. Additional accommodation and transport costs if the interruption causes you to stay longer at your destination (or the place of interruption) than originally planned.

The maximum we reimburse is stated in your Coverage Overview.

**Insured events:**

2. A family member not traveling with you becomes ill, suffers injury or gets a medical condition (this also applies if the family member contracts an epidemic or pandemic disease such as COVID-19). This only applies if the illness, injury or medical condition is life-threatening according to the doctor and hospitalization is necessary.
3. A traveling companion, family member or your service animal passes away during your trip.
4. You or a traveling companion must quarantine during your trip due to exposure to:
   a. An infectious disease, other than an epidemic or pandemic disease; or
   b. An epidemic or pandemic disease (such as COVID-19), but only if the following conditions are met:
   - The quarantine applies specifically to you or a traveling companion. This means that you or a traveling companion must be specifically and by name mentioned in an individual quarantine order due to an epidemic or pandemic; and
   - The quarantine does not apply generally: (a) to (a certain part of) a population, geographical area, building or ship (including home quarantine or similar restrictions), or (b) based on where, from where or through which country or area the person is traveling.

   a. You or a traveling companion needs medical care, or
   b. The motor vehicle must be repaired because it can no longer be driven safely.

For example, if you must be present in your capacity as a lawyer, clerk, expert witness, law enforcement officer or another profession, then this is not insured.

8. You or a traveling companion works as a first responder and is called due to an accident or emergency situation (such as a natural disaster) to provide help or support during the originally planned trip.

14. Your booked accommodation becomes uninhabitable.
15. Family outside the country where you live cannot provide you with shelter during your trip as you had planned, because someone in the household has died, become seriously ill, suffered serious injury or has a serious medical condition.

#### Search and rescue costs

Are you reported missing during your trip? Or do you need to be rescued from an emergency situation? Then we reimburse the costs of your search or rescue by a professional team. We reimburse a maximum of the amount for search and rescue costs stated in your Coverage Overview.

#### Telecommunication costs

Is there an insured event and do you have to incur telecommunication costs as a result for contact with the alarm center or a third party? Then we reimburse these costs insofar as they were necessary.

### B. Accidents

This coverage is only in effect if stated on your Coverage Overview. It will also state the amount(s) for which you are insured.

Do you have an accident during the trip, and do you die or become permanently disabled as a result? Then this is insured. We pay out a maximum of the amounts stated in your Coverage Overview. The amounts mentioned under Accidents at increased risk apply to accidents during the practice of risk sports and work activities with increased risk.

**Determination of the degree of permanent disability**
Our medical advisor determines the degree of permanent disability. They do this based on information from the treating doctor(s). Or we engage an independent doctor for this. We use the tables of the American Medical Association when we determine permanent functional disability. We take into account the degree to which you were already disabled before the accident.

**Multiple insurances**
If you have taken out multiple accident insurances with us, we will never pay out more than €125,000

**Special conditions**

1. If you die within two years after the accident as a result of it, we pay out the amount for death. Have we already paid an amount for permanent disability? Then we deduct this from it.
2. If within two years the degree of permanent disability cannot be determined? Then we have the right to postpone the payment. We then pay statutory interest on the amount we have not yet paid out.
3. We never pay out more than the insured amount. Even if it concerns multiple accidents together.
4. Is there death due to an accident? Then the heirs must allow us to have an autopsy performed on the mortal remains. Otherwise there is no coverage.
5. Have you had an accident? Then you must be examined by our medical expert. Or you are examined in a hospital that we designate.
6. A doctor must declare whether there is a chance of permanent disability. This must be within 12 months after an accident. Otherwise we cannot pay out.

3. Give us the correct and necessary information and inform yourself of all relevant important information;
4. Follow the instructions of our alarm center;
5. Comply with the law;
6. Take good care of your belongings;
7. Do everything possible to limit the damage;

### How do we determine the damage and how do we compensate it?

1. You will hear from us as soon as possible whether we will compensate the damage. And how much the compensation amounts to.
2. Fill in the (digital) claim form completely and truthfully. This way we can properly determine the damage.
3. You must be in possession of the original invoices. We can request these from you as proof.
4. Do we compensate damage to an insured object? Then we may ask you to transfer ownership to us.
5. We pay the compensations to you, unless you have let us know that these must be paid to someone else. If you pass away, the compensations are paid to the legal heirs.

1. Our claims handlers assess the damage based on the available documents. In some cases we may choose to engage an independent damage expert.

We reclaim damages in the following cases:

1. If another party is liable for your damage. Then we have the right to reclaim the damages we have paid from that other party.
2. If we find out afterwards that damage is not insured after all. Then we may reclaim the compensation.
