## 1.20 Terrorism and malicious contamination

It has become clear in the New York attack on 11 September 2001 that terrorism may have more wide-ranging and intrusive effects than was previously thought. For that reason, Dutch insurance companies have set up the Netherlands Reinsurance Company for Losses from Terrorism (NHT). In this umbrella organisation the insurance companies provide cover for the consequences of terrorism.

**NHT decision is binding**
Should you have any loss or damage related to terrorism, please inform us as soon as possible. We will submit a claim for compensation of your damage or loss at the NHT. The NHT will decide whether an event in which loss or damage occurred falls within the scope of terrorism risk. The decision of the NHT is binding for you and for us.

**Limited cover**
If the event in which your loss occurred falls within the scope of terrorism risk, the cover is limited. This limitation applies to any insurance stated on your policy schedule. The cover is limited to the amount paid to us by the NHT. The Clauses Sheet Terrorism Cover at the NHT and the Claims Settlement Protocol define the amount paid to us for compensation by the NHT. If the NHT fully compensates our claim, we are able to reimburse your loss in full.

**Extent of the cover**
You can report your loss up to two years after the decision of the NHT. Then the entitlement to cover will expire.

**More information?**
A detailed description of the term terrorism risk can be found in the Clauses Sheet Terrorism Cover at the NHT. The limitation of the cover is thoroughly explained in the Clauses Sheet. The full text of the Clauses Sheet and the Claims Settlement Protocol can be found at: www.terrorismeverzekerd.nl.

# Article 3: Continuous travel luggage insurance

These terms and conditions in this Article form an integral part of the General Terms and Conditions in Article 1.

## 3.1 Important pointers and tips

You go on holiday to enjoy the freedom, to go wherever you want. You won't let anything or anyone take that holiday pleasure away from you. The following tips may help you with this. You should be aware that you are always responsible yourself. If these tips do not apply in your situation, always look for the best possible solution.

- Take as few valuables and cash as possible with you on holiday;
- Always carry money, debit cards, cheques and travel documents with you in a 'concealed' manner in your chest or waist bag and keep as much physical contact as possible with cameras and bags.

**Are you going on holiday with a private (motor) vehicle?**

- Only load your car and/or caravan on the day of departure;
- In case of rest breaks, it is preferable to park your car in a place where you can keep an eye on it;
- Never leave valuables or valuable documents in your car, camper, truck, etc. and make sure that your other luggage is not visible from the outside in the boot;
- Always lock your car and/or caravan securely, also during rest breaks or when filling up with fuel;
- In the event of an overnight stay en route, take your luggage with you to your overnight accommodation;
- Unload your car and/or caravan on the day of arrival.

**Are you going on holiday by plane, bus, train or boat?**

- Take your valuables and valuable documents, fragile items and medicines with you in your hand luggage;
- In the event of loss of or damage to baggage checked in during air travel, always have a PIR (Property Irregularity Report) or otherwise a declaration made by the transport company, and ask for written proof.

**Are you at your destination?**

- Don't leave your valuables and valuable documents unattended in your hotel room, tent, caravan, car, on the beach or anywhere else. Use lockers if they are present. Alternatively, always carry these items with you;
- Make a note of the numbers of your travel documents such as telephone number of the emergency control room, passport or identity card, driving licence and vehicle registration document, car, travel and health insurance, bank card(s) and credit card(s). Always keep this information separate from the documents and at someone's home who you can call at any time.

**What if something goes wrong despite the above precautions?**
In the event of theft or loss of luggage, travel documents and/or money, always report this immediately to the police and ask for a copy of the police report.

## 6.2 Purpose of the cover

This cover aims to grant a benefit, in accordance with the insured component stated on the policy schedule, to the beneficiary in the event of death or permanent disability of the insured as a consequence of an accident.

## 6.3 Inclusions

In line with the definitions stated in Article 6.1 (accident) an accident also comprises:

1.  Sprain, dislocation and rupture of muscle tissue and ligaments, provided that these injuries occurred suddenly and that their nature and location can be medically ascertained, as well as skin lesions to the hands or feet caused by friction from hard objects in a relatively short period of time;
2.  Wound infection or blood poisoning that must have occurred, because pathogens have entered into the wound that is a consequence of an accident covered under the insurance;
3.  Suffocation, drowning, frostbite, sunstroke and heat stroke, burning, by corrosive liquids and a lightning strike or any other electric discharge;
4.  Exhaustion, starvation, dehydration and sunburn as a consequence of unforeseen circumstances, such as shipwreck, forced landing, collapse or a natural disaster;
5.  Complications or aggravation of accidental injuries as a direct consequence of the rendering of first aid or the medical treatment necessitated by the accident;
6.  The unintended entry of substances or objects from outside into the respiratory tract, the intestinal tract, the ear canals or the eyes which cause internal injury;
7.  Acute poisoning by ingestion of gases, fumes, solid or liquid substances;
8.  Contamination by germs as a consequence of an involuntary fall into water or another substance;
9.  Anthrax, Sarcoptes, scabies, ringworm (tricho-fyle), brucellosis and vaccinia for the insured who regularly handles cattle by virtue of his profession.

## 6.4 Aviation risk

The aviation risk, including aircraft hijacking, is covered in so far as the insured lawfully uses an airplane configured for passenger transport, while this is used for civil air traffic. Also, the risk is covered of gliding as an amateur glider pilot or as a passenger in a glider, provided that it is flown by a pilot who holds a valid glider pilot's licence.

## 6.5 Acts of war or wilful damage

During a stay outside the Netherlands this insurance provides cover for accidents as a result of acts of war or wilful damage. The company has the right to terminate the cover for acts of war midterm with a notice period of 45 days. There is no right to payment if the insured exposes himself by action or statement to any punitive measure and/or is actively involved in situations of acts of war or wilful damage.

## 6.6 Entitlement to a payment

**6.6.1 Entitlement to a payment in the event of death (A)**
In the event of death of the insured as a direct and sole consequence of an accident, the insured sum in the event of death will be paid out. In the event that a permanent disability has been granted for the same accident, the amount will be deducted from the benefit due in the event of death up to the amount not exceeding the insured sum for death. Recovery of any payment already made will not take place.

**6.6.2 Entitlement to a payment n the event of permanent disability (B)**
In the event of permanent disability of the insured as a direct and sole consequence of an accident, the payment will be set at a percentage of the insured sum for permanent disability, on the understanding that a previous payment for permanent disability, for the same accident will be deducted up to the amount not exceeding the insured sum for death. Should the insured person die, for any reason other than the accident, before the assessment of the permanent disability, then the entitlement to payment remains. The amount of the benefit is determined by the expected definitive degree of invalidity based on medical reports, if the insured had not died.

## 6.7 Determination of the degree of permanent disability

**6.7.1 Method of determining permanent disability**
The degree of permanent disability will be determined by means of a medical examination, in consultation with a physician appointed by De Goudse. The physician will be asked:
a. In case of Article 6.8.1a: the percentage of the (functional) loss of a specific body part or organ;
b. In case of Article 6.8.1b: the percentage of the loss of function of the body as a whole.
The determination of the percentage of the (functional) loss will take place in accordance with objective criteria and in compliance with the latest edition of the American Medical Association Guides.

**6.7.2 Time period for the determination of permanent disability**
The degree of permanent disability is determined, as soon as an unchanging condition can be said to exist, but in any case two years after the day of the accident.

## 6.8 Determination of the benefit in the event of permanent disability

The policyholder or, in the event of death, the person entitled to the benefit on account of death (A) shall be notified of this determination as soon as possible in each case. **6.8.2 Cumulation of the benefits**
In the event an insured has one or more accidents during the duration of this cover, the sum of all payments will not exceed the insured sum for permanent disability.

**6.8.3 Interest compensation**
If the degree of permanent disability has not yet been definitely determined six months after the day of the accident, De Goudse will compensate an annual rate of interest of 6% on the amount to be paid, starting from the seventh month after the day of the accident until the day the amount for the permanent disability will be paid out.

## 6.9 Benefit payment

De Goudse undertakes to make a payment within 30 days after its assessment. Unless otherwise agreed, the payments under this insurance will be made to the policyholder, with the exception of the payment in the event of death (A). If the policyholder and the insured are the same person, in the latter case, the payment in the event of death (A) will be made - unless otherwise agreed - to the widow/widower of the policyholder, or in his/her absence to his/her heirs.

## 6.10 Exclusions

De Goudse is not obliged to make any payment in respect of:

1.  Intent or gross recklessness of the insured person or any person who has an interest in the payment;
2.  Committing or participating in a crime or an attempt to do so;
3.  Fights or risky ventures other than a lawful manner to legitimate self-defence, rescue, or defend persons, animals or goods;
4.  Being under the influence of alcohol or by the use of medicines, intoxicating, narcotic or stimulating substances;
5.  Accidents that arise as a result of an ailing condition or as a result of physical or mental abnormalities of the insured, unless these circumstances are a result of an accident for which De Goudse, in accordance with this cover, is or was obliged to provide a payment. If, as a result, only the consequences of an accident are increased, the determination of the payment will only take into account the consequences that the accident would have had for a person without such abnormalities;
6.  By or related to nuclear reactions and the fusion products ensuing from them, irrespective of how the reactions originated, other than in the case of medical treatment of the insured that has become necessary as a result of an accident;
7.  Herniation of an intervertebral disc (hernia nuclei pulposi) and abdominal hernia. This is not considered as an accident nor as a consequence thereof, unless the medical advisor of De Goudse considers a causal link with a recent accident plausible.
8.  Subject to the provisions in Article 6.10 under 5, surgery or other medical treatment undergone with an adverse affect will also not be considered an accident;
9.  Driving a motorised vehicle for which a driving license is required, while the insured does not hold a valid driving licence;
10. Ski jumping and participation in skiing competitions;
11. Participation in equestrian competitions and show jumping;
12. Mountain and glacier trips - unless not off the beaten track or an environment without roads that is also easily accessible for untrained walkers, mountaineering and big game hunting;
13. Participating in races with bicycles or motorboats, practising sledging in mountain areas, as well as participating in speed competitions or skill contests, record or performance drives and races with motorised vehicles;
14. Practising any sport other than as an amateur.

## 6.11 Obligations in the event of an accident

**6.11.1 Period for the submission of claims**
The policyholder or the beneficiary mentioned in Article 6.8 is obliged to notify De Goudse of an accident as soon as possible, and in any case within 90 days after the accident from which a right to payment as referred to in Article 6.6.2 may arise. Should the submission be made later, a right to payment may nevertheless arise with respect to the payment referred to in Article 6.6.2, only provided that the interests of De Goudse have not been harmed and the insured/beneficiary demonstrates that no submission has been made due to extraordinary circumstances. Any right to payment, however, lapses if submission is made later than five years after the accident took place.
In the event of death (payment as referred to in Article 6.6.1) the policyholder or beneficiary is obliged to notify De Goudse of this as soon as possible, but in any event within 24 hours of the death.

**6.11.2 Obligations of the beneficiary/beneficiaries in the event of death**
In the event of death of the insured as a result of an accident the beneficiary or beneficiaries is/are obliged to cooperate with all the measures to determine the cause of death.

**6.11.3 Obligations of the insured person(s) after an accident**
The insured is obliged to:

- Undergo and stay under medical treatment immediately, if this is reasonably necessary: Do everything to promote recovery by at least following the instructions of the attending physician;
- Be examined by a physician to be designated by De Goudse, or be admitted to a hospital or other medical institution designated by De Goudse, on request and at the expense of De Goudse;
- Provide all information deemed necessary by De Goudse or have it provided to De Goudse or to the experts designated by it and not conceal any facts or circumstances that may be relevant for the determination of the degree of permanent disability;
- Notify De Goudse promptly on departure to another country of temporary residence.

**6.11.4 Obligations of the policyholder**
The policyholder is obliged to fully cooperate to comply with the obligations mentioned in Article 6.11.3.

**6.11.5 Sanction in the event of failure to comply with the obligations**
The insurance does not provide cover if the insured or in the event of death the beneficiary/beneficiaries has/have not complied with the obligations and as a result has/have harmed the interests of De Goudse.

## 6.12 Termination in the interim by the company

De Goudse has the right, without prejudice to the provisions of the General Terms and Conditions, to terminate the agreement, despite the agreed duration of the agreement, and without observing a term in the case mentioned in Article 6.13 (Amendments).

## 6.13 Amendments

**6.13.1 Obligation of notification of policyholder**
The policyholder must notify De Goudse in writing as soon as possible of any change in occupation or the related activities.

**6.13.2 The assessment of the premium and conditions after notification**
After receipt of a notification from the policyholder as referred to in Article 6.13.1, the cover will be continued unchanged, unless De Goudse indicates that it does not wish to continue the accident insurance or that it will change the premium and/or conditions to the detriment of the policyholder. If De Goudse adjusts the premium and/or conditions of the accident insurance to the detriment of the policyholder, the policyholder has the right to cancel the accident insurance by the day the amendment becomes effective, but in any case for a period of 60 days after the policyholder has been notified of the amendment. If De Goudse indicates that it wishes to discontinue the accident insurance, it will end by the termination by De Goudse with effect from the date of amendment.

**6.13.3 Consequence of not or not timely reporting**
If the policyholder fails to timely report any alterations of risk mentioned under Article 6.13.1, in case of a right to payment the following will apply:

- If, in the opinion of De Goudse, the change implies an increase in risk, the benefit will be made as normal;
- If, in the opinion of De Goudse, the change implies an increase in risk that would have led to an amendment to the conditions and/or premium, the benefit will be paid taking into account these amended conditions and/or in proportion to the premium established in the policy in relation to the new premium deemed necessary according to the company. This decrease will not be applied if the insured had an accident unrelated to his occupation;
- If, in the opinion of De Goudse, the change creates a risk that De Goudse does not want to accept, there is only a right to a payment in the event of an accident that the insured had unrelated to this occupation.

## 6.14 Termination by operation of law

With the exception of the cases referred to in Article 6.12 (Termination in the interim by the company) and Article 6.13 (Amendments), in which De Goudse has the right to discontinue the insurance or continue the insurance on amended conditions, this insurance is not subject to termination on the part of De Goudse. The insurance ends by operation of law, however, regardless of the agreed duration of the insurance, as soon as De Goudse has knowledge of the death of the insured.

## 6.15 Family accident insurance

The conditions mentioned above apply both for Personal accident insurance as for Family accident insurance. In case Family accident insurance the following additional provisions apply.

## 6.16 Hospital admissions payment

If the insured is admitted to an approved hospital as a consequence of an accident, he receives a payment per patient day in accordance with the statement of premiums and cover as long as the admission lasts a maximum of 365 days per accident. This includes the admission day and the day of discharge. After the discharge from the hospital the payment will be continued for the same number of days as the admittance to hospital lasted with a maximum of 100 days. This benefit amounts to:

- 100% of the insured sum during the first 25 days;
- 75% for the subsequent 25 days;
- 50% for the remaining 50 days.
  If the hospital admission lasts longer than 30 days, an advance payment can be granted on the payment mentioned in the first paragraph of this article.

## 6.17 Children insured under the policy

The insurance also applies to children born or officially adopted during the term of this insurance as of the date of birth, regardless of any congenital anomalies, provided that the first child is registered with De Goudse within three months after the birth or official adoption. This insurance ends for children at the end of the policy year in which the child has reached the age of 21 or as much earlier as the child marries or has an occupation.
