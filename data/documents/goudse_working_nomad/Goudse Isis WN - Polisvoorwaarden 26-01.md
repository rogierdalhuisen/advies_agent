# Prevention is better

## Know where you're going

Traveling and working abroad are wonderful experiences, but they also involve risks. You are insured for some of these with this ISIS Working Nomad. But it's naturally better to prevent unpleasant situations. For example, it's always wise to get the proper travel vaccinations; you can find more about this at ggdreisvaccinaties.nl. Additionally, it's advisable to familiarize yourself in advance with the safety and health situation at your destination, for example via the government's travel advisories (found at nederlandwereldwijd.nl).

## Watch your belongings

If you're taking baggage with you, it's important to take good care of it. If you fail to exercise sufficient care to prevent loss, theft, or damage to your belongings, you cannot rely on this insurance. You can read more about this in article 7.2.2 'Insufficient care'.

We want you to be able to enjoy your stay abroad. The following tips can help you with this.

- Consider whether you want to take your valuables with you, only do this if you need them.
- Limit the amount of cash to what's necessary.
- Always carry your money, cards, and travel documents in a 'concealed' manner while traveling, for example in a chest or waist pouch, and maintain as much physical contact as possible with cameras and bags.
- Ensure you can store your money and cards safely and securely.
- Don't leave your valuables, money, and important documents unattended in your house (hotel) room, car, on the beach, or anywhere else.
- Use safes and other well-lockable spaces. Where it's not unsafe, you can also carry your belongings with you.
- Save the alarm center's phone number in your phone (+31 182 544 555), and note down the numbers of your passport, driver's license, travel and health insurance, bank card, and credit card.
- Always keep this information separate from the original documents and also save them online.

### Are you traveling independently by car (or another vehicle)?

Only pack on the day of departure. During rest stops, park in a place where you can keep an eye on your car. Don't leave valuables, money, and important documents behind if you don't have sight of your car. Also ensure that your other baggage is not visible from outside. Always lock your car and caravan properly, even during short rest stops or when refueling. Take your baggage to your overnight accommodation when staying overnight en route. Unload your car on the day of arrival.

### Are you traveling by plane, bus, train, or boat?

Take valuables, money, important documents, breakable items, and medications in your hand baggage.

### Does something go wrong anyway?

Then immediately report to the police in case of theft of baggage, travel documents, and/or money and request a copy of the police report.

# Article 1 General

Dutch law applies to the ISIS Working Nomad.

## 1.1 Your and our expectations

### 1.1.1 What can you expect from us?

We offer excellent insurance with clear conditions. We always clearly describe what you're entitled to and what you must comply with. You can expect from us that we:

- are reasonable and fair;
- have respect for you;
- assume mutual trust;
- empathize with your situation;
- think along with you;
- handle a claim as quickly as possible.

### 1.1.2 What do we expect from you?

We expect from you that you:

- are reasonable and fair;
- have respect for us;
- pay your premium on time;
- comply with the conditions in case of damage;
- provide us with the correct information;
- do everything possible to prevent and limit damage.

## 1.2 Who is the Working Nomad insurance for?

This insurance is for Dutch nationals who are going abroad for an extended period, at least 6 months, to work and/or travel.

**Who is the insurance not for?**
This insurance does not provide coverage to:

- children younger than 1 year;
- persons going abroad as emigrants.

## 1.3 Who are insured?

On your policy schedule you can read who the insured persons are. Children younger than 1 year cannot be insured on this insurance. Children from 1 year but younger than 16 years cannot be insured without a parent or guardian as the main insured.

## 1.4 Where are you insured?

You are insured during your stay abroad. Your policy schedule states whether the coverage applies to:

- World excluding the USA
- World including the USA

With the coverage area World excluding the USA, you are insured in the USA in the following cases:

- during a stopover (max. 2 days);
- during tourist trips. You then have coverage for a maximum of 28 days per time for a stay in the USA.

When you stay in the USA for work, you only have coverage if you have insured World including the USA.

**In the Netherlands**
During a visit to the Netherlands, for the Medical costs coverage you are only insured if you do not have insurance obligation according to the Health Insurance Act. A WLZ assessment at the SVB can provide clarity about this insurance obligation.

**Are you in a risk area for which the government has issued the code red or are you in an area where there is civil commotion?**
And was this already the case when you traveled there or have you had the opportunity to leave the area after the code red was issued? Then the following limitation on coverage applies:

- The compensation for medical costs including any medical evacuation is maximum € 75,000,-.
- The compensation for the transport of the mortal remains is maximum € 10,000,-.
- There is no right to compensation in case of death due to an accident or disability due to an accident.

## 1.5 Period of validity

### 1.5.1 When does the Working Nomad insurance start and when does your coverage start?

The insurance takes effect on the start date stated on your policy schedule. Are you already abroad at the time you take out the insurance? Then a waiting period of 3 days applies. The coverage of your insurance starts on the third day after the start date. If the insurance starts on June 1, you have coverage from June 4 at 00:00. This waiting period does not apply in case of an accident.

**Duration**
Our agreement lasts one year from the start date. Upon renewal, a new insurance year begins. You will then receive a new policy. Your most recent policy shows the start date of the current insurance year. Note! The maximum duration of the insurance including renewals is ten years.

### 1.5.2 When does the Working Nomad insurance end?

The insurance ends if you cancel or if we cancel. There is then an end date. The coverage continues until and including the end date.

**If you cancel**
If you want to terminate your insurance, send an email with your policy number and the date on which your insurance must be terminated, this is never a date in the past. You can email your cancellation to your advisor or to De Goudse at expat@goudse.com.

**What applies if you cancel in the first insurance year?**
You can terminate the insurance after it has run for at least 6 months.

**If we cancel**
We may terminate the package or an insurance:

- at the end of an insurance year. You will then receive our cancellation no later than two months before the end of the insurance year. The insurance year is stated on your policy schedule;
- if you refuse to pay the initial premium or pay late. Or if you refuse to pay the subsequent premium or pay late. We will then send you a cancellation in which we state the end date. Is there a refusal to pay? Then we can cancel with immediate effect. Is there late payment? Then the end date is at least two months after the date of our cancellation;
- if we discover that you provided incorrect information when taking out or changing the insurance. And you did this to deliberately mislead us. We can then cancel with immediate effect. Within two months of the discovery, we will send you our cancellation;
- if we discover that you provided incorrect information when taking out or changing the insurance. And we would not have taken out the insurance if you had informed us correctly. We can then cancel with immediate effect. Within two months of the discovery, we will send you our cancellation;
- if we discover that you have not complied with the obligations after a claim. You can find these obligations in article 23. We will then send you our cancellation within two months of the discovery, in which we state the end date. This is at least two months after the date of our cancellation. If you have deliberately misled us after a claim, we can cancel with immediate effect.
- if you refuse to cooperate with a mandatory repatriation to the Netherlands. We can then cancel with immediate effect. Article 2.2.2 states when you are obliged to cooperate with a repatriation.

**Automatic termination of the insurance**
Depending on your age and the insured region, there may be limitations on the duration of the insurance. The main rule is that the Working Nomad insurance never runs longer than ten years. There are a few exceptions whereby the insurance ends earlier:

- An insurance with the coverage World excl. the USA can be maintained until age 55. When you have turned 55, the coverage stops as soon as the policy needs to be renewed.
- An insurance with the coverage World incl. the USA can be maintained until age 45. When you have turned 45, the coverage stops as soon as the policy needs to be renewed.
- If the insured has permanently returned to the Netherlands. This is in any case so if you have insurance obligation according to the Health Insurance Act again after your return. It's important that you report this so that the insurance is terminated and you no longer pay premium. If you don't do this within two months, you cannot claim back the premium retroactively.
- If an insured no longer has Dutch nationality or permanent residence status for the Netherlands. It's important that you report this so that the insurance is terminated and you no longer pay premium. If you don't do this within two months, you cannot claim back the premium retroactively.

If we terminate the insurance, you will receive notification of this.

## 1.6 General exclusions

We do not compensate damage that occurred:

- because you commit a crime;
- due to the possession or use of weapons by you;
- due to your intent or recklessness (see explanation below);
- due to participation in fights or quarrels;
- in connection with the use of alcohol or drugs or other (intoxicating) substances;
- due to the practice of dangerous sports;
- due to the (semi) professional practice of a sport;
- due to suicide or an attempt thereof;
- due to the performance of professional activities if special occupational or business hazards are involved. This includes offshore work, risky construction work, dangerous journalistic work, work with dangerous machines, and activities for which you lack sufficient experience, training, or expertise. Consult with your insurance advisor in case of doubt;
- due to nuclear reactions (see explanation below).

**Explanation nuclear reactions**
**No coverage:** We do not compensate damage that was caused by, occurred during, or resulted from nuclear reactions. Regardless of how these occurred.
**Coverage provided:** We do compensate damage that was caused by radioactive nuclides. These nuclides must then be located outside a nuclear installation and be used or intended for industrial, commercial, agricultural, medical, scientific, educational purposes, or for (non-military) security purposes. For this, the relevant institution, insofar as this is required, must have a permit from the government. This permit must relate to the development, use, storage, and disposal of radioactive substances. This coverage does not apply if according to a law or treaty a third party is liable for the damage suffered.

**Explanation intent**
You have no coverage if you intentionally do or fail to do something contrary to the law whereby damage occurs. The damage actually inflicted is hereby an expected or normal consequence of what you do or fail to do. Do you have no coverage? Then you also don't have it for the damage that may arise later.

**In which cases does the intent exclusion apply?**
The exclusion applies if you behave in a socially undesirable or criminal manner. This is in any case so with behaviors that can pose a danger to persons or property, such as:

- arson, destruction, and damage;
- extortion, fraud, deception, threat, robbery, embezzlement, theft, and burglary. Also if you do that with a computer or other (technical) aid;
- violence, assault, manslaughter, and murder.

This intent exclusion also applies to:

- group liability. If not you yourself but someone in a group of which you are part does or fails to do something;

## 1.7 Terrorism risk and sanctions legislation

The attack in New York on September 11, 2001 made it clear that terrorism can have much larger and more drastic consequences than was assumed until then. Insurers in the Netherlands have therefore established the Dutch Reinsurance Company for Terrorism Damage (NHT). They jointly place the coverage of the consequences of terrorism in it.

### 1.7.1 NHT makes binding statement

Do you have damage related to terrorism, report this to us as soon as possible. We then file a claim for your damage with the NHT. The NHT determines whether the event in which your damage occurred falls under the terrorism risk. The statement of the NHT is binding for you and for us.

### 1.7.2 Your coverage

If the damage arising from terrorism in a calendar year is higher than one billion for all insurers participating in the NHT together? Then the NHT establishes a payout percentage. The coverage is in that case limited to the amount that the NHT pays out to us. Does the total damage remain under one billion, or does the NHT not pay out to us for other reasons? In that case, we pay out as stated in your policy conditions.

### 1.7.3 Lapse of coverage

You can report your damage to us up to two years after the NHT has made its statement. After that, every right to coverage lapses.

### 1.7.4 Want to know more?

An accurate description of the concept of terrorism risk can be found in the 'Terrorism coverage clause sheet at the NHT'. You can also read there exactly how the limitation of coverage works. The complete text of this clause sheet and of the 'Claims settlement protocol' can be found at: www.terrorismeverzekerd.nl

## 1.9 What are the agreements for premium payment?

### 1.9.1 You pay the premium on time

To be insured, you must have paid the premium for the insurance no later than on the due date.

**What if you refuse to pay or pay late?**
If you refuse to pay the premium or pay late, the following rules apply.
Do you refuse to pay the premium? Then you are not insured. This applies to damage that occurred or was caused after the moment you refused to pay the premium.
Haven't you paid on the due date? Then you always first receive a reminder and are urgently requested to pay the premium after all. You then have 14 days for this. **Refund of premium in case of cancellation during the term**
Does the insurance end due to cancellation during the term? And have you paid premium in advance? Then we refund the premium paid too much to you. You don't get the premium back if we cancel the insurance because you have deliberately misled us.

# Article 2 SOS Assistance and medical costs

## 2.1 SOS assistance

In the coverage overview at the beginning of these conditions you can read what we compensate at most per type of SOS Assistance. Below you can read when you are entitled to compensation.
**Note:** you are only entitled to compensation if you (or someone on your behalf) first contacted the Alarm Center and received permission to incur these costs.

### 2.1.1 You pass away

**What do we compensate?**

- The transport of the mortal remains to the Netherlands or the costs of the funeral or cremation in the country of temporary stay. All costs for the transport of the mortal remains to the desired location in the Netherlands and arrange all formalities necessary for the transport. Must the mortal remains be brought to a place outside the Netherlands? Then we compensate at most the costs that would have to be incurred for transport to the Netherlands. For the funeral or cremation in the country of temporary stay, we compensate at most the amount that we would have paid for transport of the mortal remains to the Netherlands.
- The costs for the arrival of at most two persons. We compensate:
  - the travel costs (round trip), and
  - maximum ten days accommodation costs. The maximum compensation for accommodation costs and daily costs (for example for food and drinks) is € 85,- per person per day.

**What are the conditions?**

- We only compensate if you pass away outside the Netherlands due to an accident or illness.
- The persons who come over must be family members in the first or second degree or someone with whom the insured lives together permanently.
- We compensate the additional travel costs for public transport based on the lowest class.

**What do we not compensate?**
We do not compensate costs of the funeral or cremation in the Netherlands.

### 2.1.2 You become seriously ill or are involved in an accident

**What do we compensate?**

- The costs for the arrival of at most two persons. We compensate: the travel costs (round trip), and maximum ten days accommodation costs. The maximum compensation for accommodation costs and daily costs (for example for food and drinks) is € 85,- per person per day.
- Can you not or very limitedly function due to the illness or injury and therefore cannot continue your trip or your work within a reasonable period? Then we compensate the additional costs for the return trip to the Netherlands.

**What do we not compensate?**
When you have recovered after your return to the Netherlands and want to continue your trip, we do not compensate the costs for this return trip.

**What are the conditions?**

- We only compensate the additional travel costs if you have permission from the Alarm Center.
- We compensate the additional travel costs for public transport based on the lowest class, unless transport in another class is medically necessary.
- We compensate the costs of a (medical) companion if the doctor of the Alarm Center determines that this is necessary.
- The persons who come over must be family members in the first or second degree or someone with whom the insured lives together permanently.

### 2.1.3 Medical evacuation

Sometimes you need to be transported to another place because it is not possible at the place of stay to provide the medically necessary treatment. The Goudse Alarm Center then determines in which hospital this medically necessary help can be provided. You are then transported to this hospital. Even if this is a hospital in another country or in the Netherlands. We call this 'medical evacuation'.

**What do we compensate?**
We compensate the necessary costs for medical evacuation. Including the costs for medical accompaniment and for transport to the airport. We compensate the cost price.

**What are the conditions?**
The Goudse Alarm Center assesses whether the treatment and the evacuation are medically necessary and must give permission.

**What else do we compensate for medical evacuation?**
We compensate the travel costs necessary for accompanying you by another insured person. Are you traveling alone? Then we compensate the arrival of one person and maximum three days accommodation costs, so that this person can accompany you on your return journey.

**What is the condition?**
You need help for medical reasons during your return journey.

### 2.1.4 Travel costs emergency return to the Netherlands

**What do we compensate?**
We compensate the additional travel costs you must incur. We compensate your additional travel costs for public transport based on the lowest class. If you can travel back to your foreign destination within two months, we also compensate the return travel costs.

**What are the conditions for compensation of the additional travel costs?**
A family member in the first or second degree has passed away or is in mortal danger and is admitted to a hospital. We compensate the costs once per family member.

### 2.1.5 Costs of search, rescue, and recovery

**What do we compensate?**
We compensate the costs necessary for your search, rescue, or recovery if you go missing or have had an accident.

**What is the condition?**
We need an original statement from the official authority that gave the order for the search, rescue, or recovery.

### 2.1.6 Communication costs

Do you incur communication costs because an event mentioned in article 10.1 happens to you? Then we compensate these costs up to a maximum of € 200,- per event. Communication costs for contact with De Goudse Alarm Center for an event mentioned in article 10.1 are fully compensated.

## 2.2 Medical costs

The medical costs component concerns the costs of unexpected directly necessary treatments. You can claim these costs directly with us. The coverage is not the same as that of a Dutch basic health insurance and there is also no right to the Dutch healthcare allowance.

### 2.2.1 Which costs are compensated?

Do you unexpectedly become ill during your stay abroad or have an accident? Then we compensate the costs for directly necessary medical treatments. These are the costs of:

- visit to doctor or specialist;
- treatments, operations, and examinations prescribed by the doctor;
- medicines and bandages prescribed by the doctor;
- admission, treatment, and nursing in a hospital based on the lowest class (think of a multi-person room in a state hospital).
- transport to the nearest hospital or the nearest doctor, if you cannot travel independently for medical reasons;
- first prostheses that you need after an accident and that a doctor prescribes;
- the rental of aids abroad, such as crutches or a walker, that you need after an accident and that a doctor prescribes, up to a maximum of € 100,-;
- complications in the first twelve weeks of a pregnancy.

When you have returned to the Netherlands, our compensation stops, with the exception of the after-treatment costs as mentioned below.

**The maximum compensation**
The maximum compensation for:

- directly necessary treatments per event is € 2,000,000,-.
- plannable care is € 5,000,- per event.

**Important! A limited period of compensation applies to medicines.**
We compensate medicines for treating a condition arising during the duration of your insurance for 180 days. If other medicines or additional medicines are prescribed for the same condition, the day on which the first medicines were prescribed remains decisive for the period of 180 days. Is it a new condition that is not a direct result of the earlier condition? Then a new period of 180 days starts.

### 2.2.2 Obligation to cooperate with repatriation to the Netherlands

We can determine that the treatment must be carried out in the Netherlands. We call this a mandatory repatriation. If you are discharged, you must re-register in the basic register immediately after returning to the Netherlands and sign up for basic health insurance.

**What do we compensate?**
We compensate:

- the additional travel costs for public transport based on the lowest class, unless transport in another class is medically necessary.
- the costs of a (medical) companion if the doctor of the Alarm Center determines that this is necessary.

**What are the conditions?**

- The expected duration for treating and nursing a condition or injury is longer than 60 days.
- There is no increased medical risk due to the repatriation.

### 2.2.3 After-treatment costs in the Netherlands

Do you have an accident abroad for which you are treated abroad and need to be after-treated in the Netherlands? Then we compensate the necessary after-treatment costs in the Netherlands up to the maximum amount stated in the coverage overview. The costs must then be incurred within twelve months after the accident. We do not compensate transport in the Netherlands, prostheses, and aids.

### 2.2.4 Dental costs

We compensate the costs of dental care in case of damage to natural teeth due to an accident. We only compensate the costs if these are incurred within twelve months after the accident. We compensate a maximum of € 500,- per accident.

## 2.3 Exclusions SOS Assistance

You do not receive SOS Assistance compensation if there is an event:

## 2.4 Exclusions medical costs

The Medical Costs component has broad coverage. But we don't compensate everything. In addition to the exclusions of article 8, there are the following exclusions for this component.

### 2.4.1 Deductible

A deductible of € 75,- per event applies.

We do not compensate costs of treatment by a doctor or dentist or of hospital admission if the (dental) doctor or the hospital is not recognized by the competent authorities in that country at the time of treatment.

# Article 8 Accidents

## 8.1 Your accident coverage

This component only applies if it is stated on your policy schedule. You are insured for permanent disability or death if this is directly and exclusively the result of an accident that happens to you during a trip. We then pay out an amount.

### 8.1.1 Death due to an accident

If you die due to an accident, we pay out to your heirs (if you want to designate another beneficiary, this beneficiary can be stated with a clause on your policy schedule). Did you already receive a payout from us because you became permanently disabled due to that same accident? Then we offset this with the payout in case of death. The insured amount is € 30,000,-. Were you riding a motorcycle or scooter during the accident? Then the insured amount is € 2,500,-.

### 8.1.2 Permanent disability due to an accident

If you retain permanent disability from an accident, we pay out a percentage of the insured amount. We determine the amount of your payout based on the degree of permanent disability, see article 20. Have we not yet determined the amount of your payout and do you die from another cause than the accident? Then there is still a right to the payout for permanent disability. We then determine the amount of the payout based on the degree of disability that would be expected if you had not died. The insured amount is a maximum of € 100,000,-. Were you riding a motorcycle or scooter during the accident? Then the maximum insured amount is € 10,000,-.

We pay out the payout for disability to you. Do you die before we have paid out? Then there are two possibilities.

- If your death is the result of the accident, we do not pay a payout for disability but a payout for death (see above). We then pay this to your partner, or (if you don't have one) to your heirs.
- Is your death not a result of the accident? Then your partner, or (if you don't have one) your heirs, receive the payout for permanent disability instead of the death benefit.

## 8.2 Exclusions accidents

The Accidents component has broad coverage. But we don't compensate everything. In addition to the exclusions of article 1.6, there are the following exclusions for this component. You cannot rely on the Accidents component if:

1. the accident arises due to intent or with the consent of you, another insured person, or another person who has an interest in the payout;
2. the accident arises because you commit a crime, participate in a crime, or attempt to do so;
3. the accident arises because you recklessly endanger your life. We do pay out if you endanger your life because this was reasonably necessary out of lawful self-defense; or because you tried to save other people, animals, or property;
4. you have a psychological disorder of any kind. Is it medically demonstrable that this disorder is the direct result of brain damage that occurred in the accident? Then we do pay out;
5. the accident arises because the driver of a motor vehicle or vessel on or in which you sat as a passenger was under the influence of alcohol, drugs, or medicines at the time of the accident;
6. you got pathogens through an insect bite or sting. Malaria is an example of such a pathogen;
7. it concerns the consequences of a medical treatment that is not related to an accident for which you have coverage;
8. the accident arises while you are:
   - a passenger of an aircraft not used for civil air traffic;
   - a pilot of a (motor) aircraft, such as a sports plane, glider, delta glider, or sailplane;
9. the accident occurred because you:
   - were ill;
   - were in an abnormal physical condition;
   - were in an abnormal mental condition;
10. there is the following injury:
    - hernia;
    - lumbago;
    - prolapse of an intervertebral disc (hernia nuclei pulposi);
    - crepitating tendinitis (tendovaginitis crepitans);
    - muscle strain;
    - whiplash;
    - condition of the soft tissues around the shoulder joint (periarthritis humeroscapularis);
    - tennis elbow (epicondylitis lateralis);
    - golfer's elbow (epicondylitis medialis);
11. the accident arises from work with a woodworking machine.

## 8.3 What applies if you have an accident?

### 8.3.1 Death

In case of death, we assume one of the following two situations.

1. Do you die directly as a result of an accident? Or do you die later as a result of the accident, but before the percentage of permanent disability has been determined? Then the insured sum in case of death applies.
2. Do you die as a result of an accident after the percentage for permanent disability has been determined? Then from that moment the insured sum in case of death applies. Have payouts already been made in connection with permanent disability? And is the total amount higher than the payout in case of death? Then we do not reclaim this difference.

### 8.3.2 Permanent disability

The percentage of permanent disability determines the amount of the payout. Your payout is a percentage of the insured amount. The payout percentage is equal to the disability percentage, unless expressly stated otherwise. The amount of the disability percentage depends on:

- the body part or organ that was damaged in the accident;
- the state of that body part or organ: whether it has been lost or has become unusable;
- the extent to which the body part or organ has been lost or can no longer be used.

### 8.3.3 How do we determine the percentage of permanent disability?

**Fixed percentages**
Below you can read what the fixed percentage of permanent disability is in case of complete loss or loss of function of certain organs or body parts.

#### the eyesight of both eyes:

The percentage of permanent disability in case of complete loss or loss of function of the eyesight of both eyes amounts to 100%.

#### the eyesight of one eye:

The percentage of permanent disability in case of complete loss or loss of function of the eyesight of one eye amounts to 30%.

#### eyesight of one eye if we have already paid out for the loss of eyesight of the other eye

The percentage of permanent disability in case of complete loss or loss of function of the eyesight of one eye if we have already paid out for the loss of eyesight of the other eye amounts to 70%.

#### the hearing of both ears:

The percentage of permanent disability in case of complete loss or loss of function of the hearing of both ears amounts to 60%.

#### the hearing of one ear:

The percentage of permanent disability in case of complete loss or loss of function of the hearing of one ear amounts to 25%.

#### hearing of one ear if we have already paid out for complete deafness of the other ear:

The percentage of permanent disability in case of complete loss or loss of function of the hearing of one ear if we have already paid out for complete deafness of the other ear amounts to 35%.

#### an arm (including hand and fingers):

The percentage of permanent disability in case of complete loss or loss of function of an arm (including hand and fingers) amounts to 75%.

#### a hand (including fingers):

The percentage of permanent disability in case of complete loss or loss of function of a hand (including fingers) amounts to 60%.

#### a thumb:

The percentage of permanent disability in case of complete loss or loss of function of a thumb amounts to 25%.

#### an index finger:

The percentage of permanent disability in case of complete loss or loss of function of an index finger amounts to 15%.

#### a middle finger:

The percentage of permanent disability in case of complete loss or loss of function of a middle finger amounts to 12%.

#### a ring finger:

The percentage of permanent disability in case of complete loss or loss of function of a ring finger amounts to 10%.

#### a pinky:

The percentage of permanent disability in case of complete loss or loss of function of a pinky amounts to 10%.

#### a leg (including foot and toes):

The percentage of permanent disability in case of complete loss or loss of function of a leg (including foot and toes) amounts to 70%.

#### a foot (including toes):

The percentage of permanent disability in case of complete loss or loss of function of a foot (including toes) amounts to 50%.

#### a big toe:

The percentage of permanent disability in case of complete loss or loss of function of a big toe amounts to 5%.

#### another toe:

The percentage of permanent disability in case of complete loss or loss of function of another toe amounts to 3%.

#### the spleen:

The percentage of permanent disability in case of complete loss or loss of function of the spleen amounts to 5%.

#### taste and/or smell:

The percentage of permanent disability in case of complete loss or loss of function of taste and/or smell amounts to 10%.

#### a kidney:

The percentage of permanent disability in case of complete loss or loss of function of a kidney amounts to 10%.

In case of loss or loss of function of more than one finger, you never receive more payout than in case of loss or loss of function of your entire hand.

### 8.3.4 In case of partial loss or loss of function

Is it a partial loss or loss of function of the organs or body parts as mentioned above under the heading 'Fixed percentages'? Then we reduce the fixed percentages mentioned there in proportion to the degree of (function) loss.

### 8.3.5 What is the payout percentage in other cases?

Is it other cases of permanent disability than mentioned under the heading 'Fixed percentages'? Then the disability percentage is equal to the degree of loss or loss of function for the entire body.

### 8.3.6 Dental damage

Does a child under the age of 21 lose four or more permanent teeth due to an accident? Then we compensate 1% of the amount insured for permanent disability per tooth and molar lost. If the child only loses molars, then we do not compensate.

### 8.3.7 How do we determine the degree of loss or loss of function?

We determine the degree of (function) loss with the help of medical data. If medical examination is needed for this, then this examination must take place in the Netherlands. The determination of the degree of (function) loss takes place:

- according to objective standards;
- without taking occupational activities into account;
- in accordance with the latest edition at the time of determination of the 'Guides to the Evaluation of Permanent Impairment' of the American Medical Association (A.M.A.) and the Dutch guidelines established alongside or in addition to the above-mentioned edition by a scientific association of medical specialists.

### 8.3.8 Do aids count?

When determining the degree of loss, we assume the situation without external prosthetic or aids, but with any placed internal prosthetic or aids.

### 8.3.9 Within what period do we determine the payout percentage?

We determine the payout percentage as soon as there is a stable condition. But in any case within two years after the date of the accident. This does not apply if we agree something different about this with you.

### 8.3.10 Do we compensate interest?

Can we not make the payout within one year after the accident? Then we compensate the legal interest on the amount that will be paid out. We do this from the 366th day after the accident. The interest is paid out together with the payout. Is the accident not reported within three months after it occurred? And does the payout therefore take place later than would have been the case with timely reporting? Then we do not compensate interest over the period of delay.

### 8.3.11 Were you already ill or disabled before the accident?

Are the consequences of the accident increased by: an existing illness or defect; or an abnormal physical or mental condition? Then for determining the payout, we assume the consequences that the accident would have had if you had been completely healthy.

### 8.3.12 Has your illness worsened?

Has an existing illness or an existing defect become worse due to the accident? And would the accident not have happened without the existing illness or defect? Then you do not receive a payout for this.

### 8.3.13 Has your disability worsened?

Were you already disabled before the accident? Then we pay out the difference between the degree of permanent (function) loss before and after the accident. We then determine the degree of permanent (function) loss as indicated in this article.

### 8.3.14 Maximum compensation

Is there (function) loss of multiple organs or body parts with you as a result of the same accident? Then the sum of the percentages of permanent disability can never be more than 100%.

### 10.1.1 Use our damage claim form

**Note:**

We determine the damage and any additional costs that are compensated. Sometimes we have the damage determined by a damage expert whom we appoint. We convert the compensation to euros. We use the exchange rate of the date on which the event occurred. We use the exchange rate as published on the website www.oanda.com. Do we compensate to you? Then we only do this to an account that falls within the SEPA region.

Have you not complied with the obligations mentioned in these conditions? Then we can reduce the payout by the damage we have suffered as a result. However, the right to payout can then also lapse. When does the right to payout lapse? If:

- You or another person who has an interest in the payout have deliberately given us incorrect information or have withheld information that is important to us.

### 10.2.4 Does another insurance cover the damage?

Is there a right to compensation of the damage or costs from another insurance (older or younger than your insurance), a legal regulation, or another provision? Or would this right exist if this ISIS Working Nomad did not exist? Then:

- the ISIS Working Nomad applies last;
- this ISIS Working Nomad only applies above the right to compensation that has been granted, or that would have been granted, if the ISIS Working Nomad did not exist.

#### Civil commotion

Civil commotion includes:

- Armed conflict: any case in which states or other organized parties fight each other, or one the other, using military means. Armed conflict also includes the armed action of a United Nations peacekeeping force.
- Civil war: large-scale armed struggle between groups of inhabitants of the same state.
- Insurrection: organized large-scale violent resistance within a state, directed against public authority.
- Domestic unrest: large-scale violent acts occurring in different places within a state.
- Riot: a local, large-scale and violent action of a group of people, directed against the public authority of a state.
- Mutiny: a more or less organized large-scale movement of members of an armed force, directed against the authority under which they are placed. By authority is meant not only Dutch authority but also authority from the European Union, NATO, or the United Nations.

#### Accident

Sudden and unexpected violence acting on your body from the outside. This violence must have caused you to suffer bodily injury directly and in one instant. This injury must be medically determinable. The following is also understood to be an accident.

- Acute poisoning. You are acutely poisoned if you suddenly and unintentionally: took in gases; vapors; liquid or solid substances. Are you poisoned by the use of medicines or because you took in substances that caused an allergic reaction? Then we do not provide a benefit.
- Infection by pathogens or an allergic reaction. Your infection must have arisen as a direct result of an involuntary fall by you into water or another substance. Or because you entered the water or the substance to save people, animals, or property.
- The unintentional and sudden intake of substances or objects in the digestive tract, airways, eyes, or ears causing internal injury. The entry of pathogens or substances that cause an allergic reaction does not fall under this.
- Suffocation, drowning, freezing, sunstroke, and heatstroke.
- Exhaustion, starvation, dehydration, and sunburn. Only if these are the result of unforeseen circumstances.
- Wound infection or blood poisoning. This must have arisen because pathogens entered an injury. This only applies if this injury is the result of a covered accident.
- Worsening or complications of injury. This injury must be the result of a covered accident. And the worsening or complications must be a direct result of first aid. Or of the medical treatment.

#### Intent

There is intent if you do or fail to do something whereby you:

- have the intention to cause damage and/or the accident (intent as purpose);
- do not have the intention to cause damage and/or the accident, but you know for certain that damage will occur (intent with certainty consciousness);
- do not have the intention to cause damage and/or the accident, but you accept the considerable chance that damage will occur. And yet you act (do not act) so (conditional intent).
  Intent is objectively derived from the facts, circumstances, and/or your behaviors. It doesn't matter whether damage occurred to the property of this insured person or to the property of other insured persons.
