# OOM Living Abroad Insurance

## 1. Introduction

The OOM Living Abroad insurance is designed for Dutch and Belgian citizens residing, traveling, and working abroad. This flexible insurance package has an indefinite term and can be canceled daily without a notice period.

It is suitable for emigrants, expats, retirees, travelers, digital nomads, and entrepreneurs. The package offers various modules and insurance types that can be purchased individually or in combination, allowing you to customize your coverage and levels.

## 2. Coverage

The OOM Living Abroad insurance package offers the following insurance types:

1.  Health Insurance (incl. SOS)
2.  SOS (standalone)
3.  Supplementary Dental Insurance
4.  Travel Baggage Insurance & Return to NL due to family circumstances
5.  Continuous Cancellation Insurance
6.  Liability Insurance
7.  Legal Assistance Insurance
8.  Accident Insurance
9.  Contents Insurance

### Key Points per Insurance:

#### 1. Health Insurance

*   **Coverage Options:** Regular, Comfort, and Deluxe.
*   **Maximum Age:** 69 years at the time of application.
*   **Dutch Basic Insurance:** No discount applies if you are obligated to maintain basic health insurance in the Netherlands.
*   **Choice of Care Provider:** OOM generally offers free choice of care providers (hospitals, doctors) in most countries, covering both public and private healthcare.
*   **Hospital Room Costs:** OOM fully reimburses costs for a standard hospital room (multi-person room). Additional costs for a 1- or 2-person private or semi-private room are not covered to keep premiums affordable.

#### Limited Free Choice of Care Provider in Specific Countries

In the United States of America, Singapore, Malaysia, Indonesia, China, Hong Kong, Thailand, Mexico, or the United Arab Emirates, you are required to contact the OOM Assistance Center before any medical treatment. This can be done by phone at +31 70 353 2135 or via the online form on sosinternational.nl/op-reis-en-hulp-nodig. Clearly state that it is a pre-authorization request.

Medical treatment includes hospital admissions, specialist treatments, outpatient clinic treatments, and pregnancy/childbirth care.

After contacting the OOM Assistance Center, you will receive authorization for the treatment and be informed where it may take place. A payment guarantee will be issued to the healthcare provider, and OOM will settle the payment directly.

**Important:** Failure to obtain prior authorization for medical treatment in the aforementioned countries will result in no reimbursement of costs.

**Acute Care:** Prior authorization is not required for acute care. This also applies to care in countries other than those listed. Acute care is defined as care immediately (within minutes or hours) necessary to prevent serious health damage or death, such as in case of an accident or life-threatening condition.

#### Pregnancy & Childbirth

*   Coverage is provided under the Comfort and Deluxe plans.
*   A 12-month waiting period applies from the start of insurance coverage for these reimbursements.
*   With appropriate coverage and duration, a newborn child can be insured from birth without medical assessment if actively registered within 1 month of birth.
*   Refer to the Health Insurance Conditions document for full details.

#### Insurance Coverage for Temporary Stays in Specific Countries (*)

If your country of residence on the OOM policy is not one of the previously mentioned countries (*), but you are staying there temporarily (up to a maximum of three months), it is strongly recommended to adjust your health insurance via MijnOOM. Failure to do so will result in OOM reimbursing a maximum of 50% of medical costs, even for emergency care. Without adjusting your policy via MijnOOM, there is no coverage during a stay in the United States.

For stays longer than three months in these countries, a new insurance application is required, which includes a new medical acceptance procedure.

(*) United States of America, Singapore, Malaysia, Indonesia, China, Hong Kong, Thailand, Mexico, or the United Arab Emirates.

#### 2. SOS

*   SOS is an integral part of the health insurance. If health costs are not insured, SOS coverage can be purchased separately.
*   SOS covers:
    *   Arrangements and transport of remains after death.
    *   Search and rescue in case of official disappearance.
    *   Repatriation/evacuation, based on detailed conditions.

#### 3. Supplementary Dental Insurance

*   A separate dental coverage is available for higher dental costs.
*   This coverage can only be purchased in addition to OOM's Health Insurance.
*   Children up to 3 years old are covered free of charge. Premiums apply from their 4th birthday.

#### 4. Travel Baggage Insurance & Return to NL due to Family Circumstances

*   This insurance provides continuous coverage as long as you reside outside the Netherlands, including in your new country of residence.
*   Reimbursement of travel costs for an emergency return to the Netherlands due to unforeseen life-threatening family circumstances (1st and 2nd degree relatives) or death.
*   **Exclusions:** Business phones and laptops are not covered under travel baggage insurance.
*   **Maximum Baggage:** The maximum of 3000 euros for baggage applies per year, not per trip/event.
*   **Coverage Scope:** Coverage applies when staying outside your permanent residence.
*   **Important:** Travel baggage coverage does NOT reimburse medical costs or repatriation. Medical coverage must be obtained through health insurance or a separate travel insurance.

#### 5. Continuous Cancellation Insurance

*   This insurance can be purchased up to 7 days after booking. After 7 days, a clause applies stating no coverage for matters already known at the time of booking.
*   The minimum term for this insurance is 1 year.

#### 6. Liability Insurance

*   **Exclusions:** Damage caused by you as a tenant to a rented property is not covered. Liability related to motor vehicles/cars is excluded.
*   **Coverage:** Provides coverage for liability related to or around a vacant or unoccupied property in the Netherlands, provided it is not exclusively intended for rental to third parties.

#### 7. Legal Assistance Insurance

*   Covers legal assistance "in kind," meaning a legal assistance insurer in the Netherlands assigns a lawyer to help with a legal dispute.
*   Not all possible legal matters are covered; review policy conditions before purchasing.
*   **Exclusions:** The country of residence may be excluded from coverage. Employment law coverage (Excellent plan) only applies if residing and contracted in the EU + USA.
*   **Excellent Coverage:** Can only be purchased if residing in Europe or the USA.
*   **Additional Coverage:** Legal assistance for own home and rented property in the Netherlands can be additionally insured.

#### 8. Accident Insurance

*   Provides a one-time payout if you suffer permanent disability or death due to an accident.

#### 9. Contents Insurance

*   Insures your belongings in your permanent home abroad.
*   **Exclusions:** Rented contents are not covered.
*   **Limitations:** Restrictions apply to valuable items.

### Insurance Regions

The insurance has 5 regions:

*   **A World:** Global coverage.
*   **B+ World:** Global coverage except for Region A (United States).
*   **B World:** Global coverage except for Region A, B+ (United States, Singapore).
*   **C+ World:** Global coverage except for Region A, B+, B (United States, Singapore, China, Hong Kong, Malaysia, Indonesia).
*   **C World:** Global coverage except for Region A, B+, B, and C+ (United States, Singapore, China, Hong Kong, Malaysia, Indonesia, Thailand, United Arab Emirates, Mexico).

**Explanation:**

*   Region C is the most affordable, Region A is the most expensive.
*   Choose Region A if you require coverage in the USA. For sporadic travel to the USA, consider temporary additional coverage.
*   Coverage in a higher region is limited. If you frequently travel to a country in a higher coverage region, consult to determine the best region choice.
*   **Care in the United States:** If you are insured for Region B+, B, C+, or C, you will not receive reimbursement for medical costs in the United States.
*   **Care in Other More Expensive Regions:** If you are insured for Region B, C+, or C and incur costs in a higher region (excluding the United States), OOM will only reimburse 50% of the costs, provided they fall under your coverage.

## 5. After Application of the Insurance

### When Does Coverage Start?

*   There is no insurance agreement until the application is accepted.
*   Until an agreement is reached, medical issues (changes in health status) must be reported to OOM (Disclosure Duty*). OOM will consider these new circumstances in the assessment.
*   Only after an insurance policy is explicitly accepted (you receive a policy or agree to a proposal):
    *   You have certainty that the insurance will start on the agreed effective date. For proposals based on medical situations, OOM will not start the insurance earlier than the date you received the proposal.
    *   Changes in health status no longer need to be reported; from the effective date of the OOM insurance, these medical issues are also covered.

Therefore, it is recommended to submit an application as early as possible, up to 2 months before the desired effective date.

*Disclosure Duty during the application process:*
*   OOM provides a special document explaining the disclosure duty.
*   You must complete all information truthfully during the application.
*   Changes in your situation (until acceptance) must also be actively reported to OOM.

**IMPORTANT:** Coverage (from the agreed effective date) only begins once there is an agreement or policy document from OOM Verzekeringen.
