# Goudse Expat Package

The Goudse Expat Package is a comprehensive international insurance package specifically designed for Dutch and Belgian nationals who emigrate abroad to live and work, or who are already residing abroad.

This package offers extensive coverage for healthcare costs, including coverage in private clinics and single rooms (Excellent coverage level) and medical repatriation. Additionally, the Goudse Expat Package includes various other important insurances such as travel, travel baggage, cancellation, disability, liability, household contents, legal assistance, and accidents.

This expat insurance from De Goudse is a flexible, continuous insurance, specially designed for Dutch and Belgian nationals working abroad for an extended period. This applies whether you are seconded by a (foreign) employer, have found local employment, or are living abroad as a freelancer or self-employed entrepreneur. You can apply for the insurance for yourself and your family members. Dutch employers can also take out this insurance for their employees abroad.

## 3. Points of Attention

### End Age
As of 2022, the end age for this package has been raised to 72 years (previously 67 years). However, for the healthcare component, limitations apply from age 67.
*   For insured persons aged 67 and older, a lower maximum insured amount of €100,000 per insured person per event applies, instead of €2,000,000.
*   In case of an accident and for the United States region, the reduced amount is €150,000.
*   After the 72nd birthday, coverage will not be extended. Coverage therefore ends between the 72nd and 73rd birthday. The exact termination date is the date on which the insurance would normally be renewed (see policy schedule).

### Exclusions
Note that not "everything" is covered. This includes professions with increased risk, competitive sports, dangerous sports. Contact us in advance to inquire whether a profession and/or sport is covered. You can also check this in the detailed conditions. Additionally, there are the usual exclusions, such as alcohol and drug abuse and criminal activity. Always read the conditions carefully to avoid misunderstandings and incorrect expectations.

### Free choice of healthcare provider and coverage in private clinics
De Goudse offers 'free choice of healthcare provider'. Free choice means you have the choice of both doctor and hospital within the region where your country of residence is classified by De Goudse AND within the region(s) for which lower premiums apply at De Goudse. Private clinics are also covered by the insurance.

De Goudse requires that prior to a planned hospital or clinic admission, telephone contact must be made with De Goudse's emergency center. During this call, you can indicate where you would like to be treated, and De Goudse will administratively guide the admission. This means, among other things, that they will try to arrange a payment guarantee where possible, so you do not have to pre-finance hospital admission costs yourself.

*De Goudse reserves the right to offer you an alternative good hospital for your chosen medical facility in case of an exorbitant exceeding of the expected healthcare costs (the so-called "reasonable and customary costs"). This is compared to the average costs in good hospitals in your region. In practice, this is very rarely used. De Goudse's primary concern is to prevent abuse (deviantly charging extremely high costs to expats) and to control costs/premiums while prioritizing good healthcare.*

### Coverage for 1- or 2-person rooms
*   **Standard and Optimal Coverage Levels**: Coverage for a 2-person room (semi-private room). If only a 1-person room is available, De Goudse has a separate arrangement for this.
*   **Excellent Coverage Level**: Coverage for a standard* 1-person room (private room).

*For more luxurious care than standard, this often involves extras such as more extensive care, better food, a larger television, the view, etc. De Goudse insures the actual healthcare costs for good care, and any luxurious 'hotel-like facilities' are at your own expense.*

### USA (United States of America)
Different rules apply to the USA. The USA is a highly regulated country in terms of medical care, and healthcare costs are very high compared to the rest of the world. In the USA, De Goudse therefore collaborates with a local emergency center (GMMI), which you must contact in advance for planned admissions. In consultation with GMMI, it will then be determined where you can undergo treatment. However, you can decide to go to a doctor and hospital of your own choice without GMMI's mediation, but in that case, you will only be reimbursed 80% of your healthcare costs by De Goudse.

#### Essential Health Benefits (Obamacare)
These are coverages that a health insurance policy in the United States must meet. The American law, Affordable Care Act (ACA), stipulates that someone permanently residing in the United States must have health insurance that meets the Essential Health Benefits. De Goudse is one of the few providers of expat insurance that offers a product (Excellent USA coverage) that is compliant and thus meets the requirements of the so-called Minimum Essential Care benefits (MEC). Note that for the Excellent USA coverage, in addition to the regular documents, the "Addendum Essential Health Benefits for the United States" document also applies.

### Direct Billing Healthcare Costs
Direct billing is the international insurance term for 'direct payment' and means that the insurer settles healthcare costs directly with the healthcare provider (often the hospital). This way, you do not have to pre-finance large amounts yourself. Compared to the 'insurance market', De Goudse applies relatively low limits for direct billing and does not distinguish between outpatient and inpatient costs. Up to an amount of €1000, you must pay the invoice to the healthcare provider yourself and claim it from De Goudse afterwards. If the €1000 is expected to be exceeded, a payment arrangement can be set up via De Goudse's emergency center so that you do not have to pre-finance large amounts yourself. Direct billing only applies to healthcare costs, not to the other insurances in the Goudse Expat Package.

### Tenant's Liability
Within the Household Contents Insurance of the Expat Package, it is possible for tenants of a home in Europe to take out tenant's liability insurance. This option arises from local legislation (e.g., in Belgium, Luxembourg, France, Switzerland, and Italy) where tenants of a home in those countries can be held liable for damage to the property.

De Goudse's coverage for tenant's liability is capped at €200,000 per event. This is an important fact because if you rent a house with a reconstruction value of €500,000, you will be short €300,000 in coverage for reconstruction after, for example, a major fire. This is a significant risk that we therefore strongly warn against.

If you are going to rent in a country where you can be held liable for damage to the rented property, we strongly advise you to also look into local options to insure this risk. The option offered by De Goudse is too limited to provide adequate coverage in all cases. We do advise you to always take out insurance for this risk, as it potentially involves substantial claims. Damage to real estate is not covered by liability insurance.

The Goudse Package offers various discount and benefit possibilities:

#### 1. Discount in connection with Dutch basic health insurance (not applicable to the Belgian healthcare system)
In certain situations (e.g., secondment), Dutch basic health insurance remains active during a stay abroad. In that case, De Goudse offers you discount possibilities of 10% to a maximum of 20% on the premium for the Healthcare component. The amount depends on your country of residence and coverage area:
*   20% Europe & Worldwide excl. (Canada, China, Hong Kong, Japan, Singapore, UAE, and USA)
*   10% Canada, China, Hong Kong, Japan, Singapore, UAE, and USA

This discount can be applied per insured person. Sometimes, the basic insurance remains active only for the working individual, and not for family members.

#### 2. Discount in connection with a local health insurance
If you have coverage in your country of residence through a national system or an employer, you can receive a 10% discount on the premium for the healthcare component. This discount is subject to assessment and depends on the content of your local insurance. Please send the available information about your insurance by email so we can assess whether you qualify for a discount. You can also use this retrospectively if your insurance with De Goudse is already active.

#### 3. Family Discount and Benefits
Insuring multiple people within one family on one policy offers several advantages:
*   A favorable family premium for SOS, Travel Baggage and Cancellation Costs, Legal Assistance, Private Liability, and Family Accidents.
*   Only 1 general deductible (€0, €250, €500, €1000, and in expensive regions €2500 per year) applies to all insured persons on the policy for the healthcare component. This is very favorable compared to almost all other insurers who charge a deductible per person per year.
*   In case of a life-threatening illness or death of a 1st or 2nd degree family member in the Netherlands or Belgium, the SOS insurance provides coverage for return to the Netherlands or Belgium for all insured persons on the policy.
