# 1. How does health insurance work in practice?

If you have taken out the health insurance with mandatory SOS coverage in the insurance package from De Goudse, various situations may arise where it is necessary to contact organizations involved in the delivery of services. These are the following four organizations, and we would like to explain what they do and when you can or must use them:

1. Air Doctor
2. De ANWB (Emergency Centre)
3. De Goudse Verzekeringen
4. JoHo Insurances

## 1) Air Doctor

Air Doctor is a globally operating organization that you can contact online to find local (general) practitioners and specialists for making and being reimbursed for an appointment for non-emergency care, including when traveling outside your country of residence. The back of your policy schedule explains how you can use Air Doctor. There are a few points of attention:

1. In case of emergency / urgency and/or hospitalization, call a local ambulance if necessary and call the emergency center (the ANWB – see explanation below) and do NOT consult Air Doctor. Air Doctor is only to be used for non-emergency medical matters!
2. You are not obliged to use Air Doctor. If you need a doctor, you may also find a (general) practitioner yourself, pay the medical costs invoice directly, and claim it afterwards from De Goudse.

**Examples where you can contact Air Doctor:**

- Making an appointment with a GP for headache complaints (non-emergency).
- Making an appointment with a dentist for a check-up / cleaning (Note! costs are only reimbursed if dental care is insured on your policy with De Goudse).
- Appointment with a specialist for a consultation regarding knee complaints (non-emergency).

## 2) ANWB - the emergency center of De Goudse (+31 182 544 557)

The ANWB is the emergency center of De Goudse and is available 24/7. The ANWB carries out assistance for the insured on behalf of De Goudse. Due to their activities, this may also concern medical data. The ANWB is authorized for this and handles your data according to Dutch laws and regulations. It is important for you to know what the emergency center does and what it does not do, so that you can go to the right instance with your questions:

**What the emergency center DOES do:**

In the following situations, you must – always in advance – contact De Goudse Emergency Center.

- Non-emergency hospitalization.
- Emergency hospitalization: contact as soon as possible (potentially by a family member).
- Direct payment of medical costs (if > 1,000 euros) to the healthcare provider.
- After diagnosis for a long-term treatment path (for example, after a cancer diagnosis).
- In case of pregnancy, before the first ultrasound takes place (see also elsewhere in this document).
- If you want advice on finding a healthcare provider.
- If specific care is not available locally, you need to be moved, and cannot travel yourself for medical reasons.

The information below is only relevant if you (or your partner) are also insured for pregnancy. This is the case with Optimal and Excellent coverage.

## 1) Report to the Goudse Emergency Center

It can vary by country what is customary: giving birth at home or in a hospital. In both cases, it is wise to choose a hospital where you want to give birth in advance. If you are allowed to choose between a home birth or hospital birth yourself, we also recommend contacting the emergency center for approval. - medical advice
- guarantee of payment to the hospital

## 5) Insuring the newborn child

You can register your newborn child via email after birth to be included in your insurance. Send us an email containing:

1. Date of birth
2. First names and surname
3. Gender
4. Nationality

If you register the child within 1 month after birth, the child will be fully insured from the date of birth without medical questions. If you register the child later, De Goudse may ask medical questions about the child's health and, based on that, potentially impose restrictions or even reject the registration.

**Important**: coverage for the child includes reimbursement for medical costs incurred for the child immediately after birth. If you do not immediately insure the child, you run the risk of having to pay the costs for the child yourself. For this reason, we always advise insuring the child from birth.

A premium will be charged for children, which is customary in private health insurance. See the premium and coverage overview for exact costs.

# 4. Changing health insurance coverage

If you have taken out health insurance, you might want to know how it works if you want to adjust the coverage. You chose a coverage level of Standard, Optimal, or Excellent on the health insurance. Should you wish to change the coverage in the future, please keep the following in mind:

- Coverage can only be adjusted for all health insured persons on the policy.
- To increase coverage, De Goudse requires a new, current health declaration. De Goudse assesses your request based on the answers in this health declaration and reserves the right to reject the request or apply clauses.
- A decrease in coverage can be done without a health declaration, but if you want to upgrade again later, you will have to provide a new health declaration.
- Waiting periods remain applicable (unless they already applied to the coverage in the current insurance coverage and had already expired).
- Changing coverage can be done once per year and only as of the insurance renewal date (see the policy for this date), but not retroactively.
- Send us an email at contact@johoinsurances.org and ask us to assist you in adjusting your insurance.

# 5. Changing health insurance deductible

A number of things also apply when changing the deductible (eigen risico) on your policy:

- The deductible applies per family per year and runs from the annual renewal date until the renewal date a year later (this is different from, for example, the NL basic insurance which has a term from 1/1 – 31/12).
- The deductible applies to all types of health costs you incur and claim, so both for hospitalization and consultation with a specialist or medication, for example.
- The deductible can only be adjusted for all health insured persons on the policy and for all persons per annual renewal date of your insurance.
- For changing a deductible (increase or decrease), De Goudse does NOT ask for a new health declaration.
- If you want to lower your deductible from 250 euros to zero euros per year, it costs 170 euros extra premium per insured adult.
- Taking the premium structure into account, it is relatively more advantageous for 2 insured adults on the policy to choose the deductible of 500 euros.
- Changing coverage can be done once per year and only as of the insurance renewal date (see the policy for this date), but not retroactively.
- Send us an email at contact@johoinsurances.org and ask us to assist you in adjusting your insurance.

# 6. Adding an insured person to your policy

For various reasons, a family member/partner may be added to the policy at a later time (after the start date). How this works differs per insurance component:

## 1) Health costs (incl SOS)

For the person to be insured, it is necessary to fill out a health declaration. You send this health declaration to us, or if preferred, directly to the medical department of De Goudse. The declaration will be assessed by the medical advisor of De Goudse. We monitor this application process. If there are questions resulting from the health declaration, the insurer will contact you directly. An exception to this procedure is the registration of a newborn child. Provided registration occurs within 30 days after birth, a newborn child is automatically insured without medical questions if the parents are already insured for health costs.

After the insured person is added, the additional premium will be charged by the insurer. The co-insured on the policy must always have the same medical coverage as the other persons already insured. The SOS insurance will also be adjusted to the correct family composition if necessary.

## 2) Dental care

Dental care coverage can be added per person. For invasive dental care (see conditions), a waiting period of 1 year applies.

## 3) Liability, Travel insurance, Legal assistance, SOS, Accidents

Send us an email with the request to add someone to your policy. The premium changes if the family composition changes\*.

\*The Travel component (baggage + cancellation) is a policy coverage (valid for everyone on the policy); the premium does not change if someone is added to the policy.

## 4) Home contents

Send us an email with the request to add this coverage to your policy, including the answers to the questions asked in the home contents section of the application form.

# 7. Coverage outside the country of residence and during travel and vacations

## SOS and Health costs

If you have included SOS and Health insurance in this insurance package, you have worldwide coverage for unforeseen emergency medical costs during travel and vacations. There is a maximum of 120 days of coverage in a more expensive region (see policy schedule).

## Coverage for non-emergency and planned medical assistance outside your region

Your country of residence is classified into a premium region. There are 4 premium regions regarding health insurance; this is explained on your policy. Undergoing planned care in a more expensive premium region than the one your country of residence belongs to is not covered. If you actually go to live in another country, you must report this to us and we will have the country of residence changed on the policy.

## 8. Ministry of Foreign Affairs Travel Advice

The travel advice from the Ministry of Foreign Affairs can affect your coverage (even if you have Belgian nationality) if there is a “red” travel advice:

> "Very serious security risks. Do not travel to this country or area. Are you traveling anyway? Prepare yourself very well. Due to very serious security risks, it is too dangerous there."

The conditions describe the influence the advice has on the coverages in this insurance package. More information about the ministry's travel advice can be found:

- On the website www.nederlandwereldwijd.nl
- In the Travel app of the ministry.

Tip: in the Travel app, you can set notifications for one or more countries where you regularly stay. You will then automatically receive a message if a travel advice changes.

## 10. Duration and termination of your insurance.

Your insurance is automatically renewed annually, unless you wish to stop the insurance yourself, of course. In a number of situations, your insurance will mandatorily end:

- In situations such as non-payment, fraud, concealment (see the general conditions);
- Upon final return to the Netherlands or Belgium;
- On the last day of the insurance year in which the insured person turns 72. NOTE: from age 67, limited coverage applies to the health costs component. Read the conditions for the details of this limitation.
- If a policyholder (also) obtains the nationality of the country of residence.
